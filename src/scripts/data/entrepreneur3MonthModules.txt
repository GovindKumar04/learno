Domain

Month 1 - Financial Foundations & Modeling Basics
> Financial Statements for Startups
  P&L statement: revenue lines, COGS, gross margin, operating expenses (burn), EBITDA, and net loss -- what each line means for a startup
  Balance sheet: assets (cash, AR, inventory, fixed assets), liabilities (debt, AP, deferred revenue), equity (founder shares, investor capital, retained earnings)
  Cash flow statement: operating (day-to-day), investing (capex), financing (fundraising, debt repayment), and free cash flow
  Financial ratios for startups: gross margin %, burn multiple (net burn / net new ARR), quick ratio, and CAC payback period
  Common startup accounting mistakes: mixing personal/business finances, not tracking deferred revenue, and ignoring equity dilution impact
> Excel Financial Modeling
  Excel fundamentals for finance: named ranges, OFFSET, INDEX/MATCH, IFERROR, and array formulas for dynamic models
  Model architecture: assumption sheet → revenue sheet → cost sheet → P&L → balance sheet → cash flow (linked, not hardcoded)
  Revenue model types: subscription (MRR/ARR), transactional (volume × price), marketplace (GMV × take rate), and SaaS seats
  Scenario analysis: data tables (one-variable, two-variable), scenario manager, and sensitivity analysis for key assumptions
  Model auditing: precedent/dependent tracing, formula consistency checks, and using color coding (blue = input, black = formula)

Month 2 - Advanced Modeling & Valuation Methods
> Startup-Specific Financial Models
  SaaS metrics model: MRR waterfall (new + expansion - contraction - churn), ARR, NRR (net revenue retention), and LTV/CAC model
  Unit economics deep dive: cohort analysis in Excel, calculating blended vs. marginal CAC, and payback period by channel
  Three-statement model: linking P&L to balance sheet (retained earnings) and cash flow (net income + non-cash + working capital)
  Fundraising model: pre-money/post-money valuation, SAFE mechanics (discount rate, valuation cap, MFN clause), and dilution table
  Burn rate modeling: runway calculation, hiring plan impact on burn, and sensitivity analysis for different growth scenarios
> Startup Valuation Methods
  Venture Capital method: post-money valuation = exit value / expected ROI, and back-calculating pre-money valuation
  Comparable company analysis (comps): selecting peer companies, EV/Revenue multiples for SaaS (ARR multiple), and adjusting for growth
  DCF for startups: why traditional DCF is hard (no positive cash flows), terminal value dominance, and using scenario-weighted DCF
  Scorecard valuation (pre-revenue): comparing startup to median pre-money, scoring on team/market/product/traction factors
  First Chicago method: three scenarios (success, sideways, failure) × probability × exit value = weighted valuation

Month 3 - Investor Decks, Due Diligence & Capstone
> Investor Financial Presentation
  Financial slide in pitch deck: 3-year forecast (revenue, gross margin, EBITDA, cash), key metrics dashboard, and use of funds
  Data room preparation: audited or reviewed financials, MIS reports, board minutes, cap table, and contracts
  Due diligence financial checklist: revenue recognition policy, AR aging, deferred revenue schedule, and customer concentration risk
  Virtual data room (VDR) setup: DocSend (analytics + NDAs), Notion, Google Drive with permission tiers, and folder structure
  Financial Q&A prep: practicing answers to "How did you arrive at this forecast?", "What are your key assumptions?", "What does your downside look like?"
> Capstone - Complete Financial Model
  Build a full 3-statement financial model for a mock SaaS startup from scratch using provided assumptions
  Perform valuation using VC method and comps -- arrive at a defensible pre-money valuation range
  Create a 5-slide financial presentation: business model summary, unit economics, 3-year forecast, valuation, and use of funds
  Stress-test the model: what if churn doubles? What if CAC increases by 50%? What if fundraising is delayed by 6 months?
  Present model to a mock "investor panel" (peers + instructor) and handle tough financial due diligence questions

Month 1 - Operations Fundamentals & Process Design
> Startup Operations Foundations
  Operations defined: the system that converts inputs (money, people, materials, time) into outputs (products, services, value) reliably
  Operations vs. strategy: strategy is what to do, operations is how to do it reliably at scale -- and why most startups fail at operations
  Process mapping: swimlane diagrams (who does what when), process flow diagrams, and identifying bottlenecks (Theory of Constraints)
  KPIs for operations: throughput, cycle time, defect rate, on-time delivery, and operational efficiency ratio (output/input)
  Operations tech stack for startups: Notion/Trello (process docs), Slack (communication), Zapier (automation), and Monday.com (project ops)
> Supply Chain Basics
  Supply chain anatomy: supplier → manufacturer → distributor → retailer → customer, and where value is added/lost at each node
  Make vs. buy decision: total cost analysis (unit cost + overhead + quality + lead time + strategic control), and when to outsource
  Supplier selection: RFQ (Request for Quotation) process, vendor scorecard (quality, cost, delivery, responsiveness), and multi-sourcing
  Inventory basics: EOQ (Economic Order Quantity), reorder point (ROP = lead time demand + safety stock), and ABC analysis
  India-specific supply chain: MSME vendor ecosystem, Government e-Marketplace (GeM), and leveraging SIDBI supply chain financing

Month 2 - Logistics, Quality & Technology
> Logistics & Fulfillment
  Last-mile delivery in India: Delhivery, Shiprocket, Ecom Express -- comparison of rates, RTO%, and API integration capability
  Warehouse operations: layout design (ABC zoning), pick-pack-ship process, WMS (Warehouse Management System) basics
  Returns management (reverse logistics): return rate benchmarks by category, return processing cost, and refurbishment/resale strategy
  Freight and shipping: domestic (road, rail, air -- cost vs. speed), international (Incoterms -- FOB, CIF, DDP), and customs documentation
  3PL vs. in-house fulfillment: decision matrix (volume, SKU count, geographic reach, capital availability), and 3PL contract negotiation
> Quality Management
  Quality management systems: ISO 9001 framework, PDCA cycle (Plan-Do-Check-Act), and Six Sigma DMAIC for process improvement
  Statistical Process Control (SPC): control charts (X-bar, R-chart), control limits vs. specification limits, and detecting process drift
  Root cause analysis tools: 5 Whys (simple, iterative), Fishbone diagram (cause-effect for complex problems), and Fault Tree Analysis
  Supplier quality management: incoming quality control (IQC), Approved Vendor List (AVL), and supplier audit process
  Quality culture: building quality-first mindset in team, cost of poor quality (COPQ), and using quality data for continuous improvement

Month 3 - Scaling Operations & Capstone
> Scaling Operations
  Scaling challenges: what breaks at 10x volume (people, process, systems), and the "scaling valley of death" for startups
  Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs): writing effective SOPs (purpose, scope, steps, owner, frequency), and SOP management system
  OKRs for operations: setting quarterly operational OKRs, cascading from company to team to individual, and weekly check-ins
  Tech-enabled operations: ERP systems for SMEs (Zoho ERP, Odoo), when to implement ERP, and avoiding premature ERP complexity
  Automation roadmap: identifying automation candidates (repetitive, rule-based, high-volume tasks), RPA tools (UiPath, Automation Anywhere)
> Capstone - Operations Improvement Project
  Select a real or mock startup with an operational problem (high RTO%, slow fulfillment, quality defects, supplier issues)
  Map the current state process (as-is), identify waste and bottlenecks using lean tools, and design the future state (to-be)
  Develop a supply chain strategy: make/buy decisions, supplier selection, inventory policy, and logistics model
  Create an ops tech stack recommendation: specific tools, integration architecture, implementation timeline, and ROI calculation
  Present the complete operations transformation plan with before/after KPI projections to a mock board


Course #E28: Product Management for Non-Tech Founders
Domain

Duration

Month 1 - Product Thinking & Discovery
> Product Management Fundamentals
  PM role defined: the intersection of business (why), tech (how), and user experience (what) -- and why non-tech founders often make great PMs
  Product vs. project: product is ongoing (evolves with users), project is finite (defined scope, end date) -- and why confusing them kills startups
  Product thinking: starting with the problem (not the solution), defining the user, the problem, and the desired outcome
  Jobs-to-be-done (JTBD) framework: what "job" is the user hiring your product to do? Milkshake example and startup applications
  Product-market fit signals: retention curves flatten, NPS > 40, users are "very disappointed" without product (Sean Ellis survey)
> User Research Methods
  User interview framework: recruiting (5-7 users per segment), interview guide (open-ended, past behavior, not hypothetical), and synthesis
  Observation methods: contextual inquiry (watch users in their environment), usability testing (task-based), and diary studies
  Survey design: Likert scale pitfalls, question ordering bias, and validated survey tools (NPS, CSAT, CES -- Customer Effort Score)
  Persona creation: data-driven personas (from research, not assumptions), anti-persona (who you're NOT building for), and jobs map
  Competitive analysis: feature matrix, positioning map, and identifying whitespace (unmet needs your competitors are ignoring)

Month 2 - Roadmapping, Prioritization & Design
> Product Roadmap & Prioritization
  Roadmap types: now-next-later (flexible, outcome-based), timeline roadmap (for stakeholders), and feature roadmap (for engineering)
  Prioritization frameworks: RICE (Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort), MoSCoW (Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, Won't-have), Kano model
  User story writing: "As a [user], I want to [action] so that [outcome]" -- acceptance criteria, and definition of done
  Backlog management: epic → user story → task hierarchy, sprint planning, and balancing new features vs. tech debt vs. bug fixes
  Stakeholder management: saying no (kindly), managing founder/investor feature requests, and using data to defend prioritization decisions
> UX/UI for Non-Designers
  UX principles: affordance (visual cues for actions), consistency, feedback (system status), error prevention, and accessibility
  Information architecture: card sorting, navigation design, and creating site maps for web/mobile apps
  Wireframing with Figma: low-fidelity wireframes (boxes and lines), component libraries, and collaborative design for non-designers
  Prototyping: clickable prototypes in Figma, prototype testing (usability sessions), and iterating on feedback
  Design handoff to developers: Figma inspect mode, design tokens (colors, typography, spacing), and developer Q&A process

Month 3 - Product Launch, Metrics & Capstone
> Product Launch & Growth
  Go-to-market for product features: internal beta (dogfooding), closed beta (selected users), and public launch sequencing
  Launch checklist: support documentation, in-app onboarding (tooltips, walkthroughs), sales enablement, and PR/marketing coordination
  Feature adoption metrics: activation rate (users who complete key action), adoption rate (% of users using new feature), and engagement depth
  A/B testing: hypothesis formation, sample size calculation, statistical significance, and interpreting inconclusive results
  Product-led growth (PLG): freemium model, viral loops (referral, collaboration features), and optimizing the self-serve onboarding funnel
> Capstone - Product Spec & Roadmap
  Write a complete Product Requirements Document (PRD) for a new product feature: problem statement, user research summary, goals, user stories, scope, and success metrics
  Create a 6-month product roadmap with priorities, rationale, and business impact estimates for each initiative
  Build a product metrics dashboard: define North Star metric, supporting metrics, and leading indicators for a mock product
  Conduct a mock usability test: create tasks, recruit 3 users, run sessions, synthesize findings, and present recommendations
  Present the complete product strategy to a mock board: vision, roadmap, resource ask, and expected business outcomes

Month 1 - Export Fundamentals & Documentation
> Export Business Foundations
  India export opportunity: India exports $450B+ annually, top categories (engineering goods, pharma, textiles, IT services), and finding your product niche
  Export readiness checklist: IEC (Import Export Code from DGFT), GST registration, current account in bank for foreign remittance, and RCMC (Registration cum Membership Certificate)
  Export market research: using DGFT trade data, ITC-HS code lookup, top importing countries for your product, and IBEF country reports
  Pricing for export: cost-plus pricing (production cost + profit + freight + insurance), market-based pricing, and understanding Incoterms impact on price
  Export promotion councils: APEDA (agriculture), FIEO (general), EPC for textiles/gems/pharma -- benefits, membership, and government scheme access
> Export Documentation
  Core export documents: Commercial Invoice (price, quantity, parties), Packing List (weight, dimensions, package count), Bill of Lading/Airway Bill (transport contract)
  Customs documents: Shipping Bill (export declaration in ICEGATE), Certificate of Origin (for FTA benefits), and ARE-1 form (for excise-free exports)
  Certificate of Origin: self-certification (for some FTAs), Authorized Economic Operator (AEO), and country-specific CO requirements
  Letter of Credit (LC): LC at sight vs. usance LC, LC terms (partial shipments, transshipment), and how to read and comply with LC conditions
  Trade finance documents: Bill of Exchange, Export Packing Credit (Pre-shipment finance from bank at concessional rate), and ECGC insurance

Month 2 - Logistics, Buyers & Market Entry
> Export Logistics
  Freight modes: sea freight (FCL vs. LCL -- cost, lead time, min volume), air freight (faster, higher cost, for high-value/perishable goods), and courier (DHL, FedEx -- for samples, small shipments)
  Freight forwarder selection: IATA-certified, experience in your product/destination, customs broker network, and end-to-end visibility platform
  Incoterms 2020: EXW (factory pick-up, buyer risk), FOB (seller until vessel), CIF (seller pays freight+insurance), DDP (seller pays all) -- when to use each
  Customs clearance at destination: HS code classification, import duties (MFN rate vs. preferential rate under FTA), and anti-dumping duties (if applicable)
  Export packaging: ISPM 15 (wood packaging fumigation requirement), product labeling requirements by country (FDA for US, CE for EU), and packaging for sea transport (humidity, shock)
> Finding Buyers & Market Entry
  Digital channels for buyer discovery: Alibaba.com (supplier profile, RFQ response), Global Sources, IndiaMART for international leads, and LinkedIn for B2B
  Trade fairs and exhibitions: Hannover Messe (engineering), SIAL (food), Magic (apparel) -- how to exhibit cost-effectively (FIEO subsidies available)
  Buyer qualification: checking importer credibility (Dun & Bradstreet, credit reports), trade references, and starting with small trial orders
  Distributor vs. direct sales: distributor (faster market entry, local expertise, lower control), direct (higher margin, relationship ownership, more investment)
  FTA leverage: India-UAE CEPA (0% duty for 97% goods), India-Australia ECTA, GSP benefits for US/EU -- calculating duty savings and competitive advantage

Month 3 - Finance, Compliance & Capstone
> Export Finance & Compliance
  Export payment methods: Advance payment (safest for exporter), LC (secure, complex), Documents against Payment (D/P), Open Account (riskiest for exporter)
  Foreign exchange management: FEMA compliance, realization of export proceeds (within 9 months), and ECB (External Commercial Borrowings) regulations
  Government export incentives: RoDTEP (Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products) -- rates by HS code, MEIS replacement, and claiming online
  ECGC (Export Credit Guarantee Corporation): buyer default insurance, political risk insurance, and bank guarantee for post-shipment credit
  GST on exports: zero-rated supply, LUT (Letter of Undertaking) filing for exports without IGST payment, and IGST refund process (automated vs. manual)
> Capstone - Export Business Plan
  Select a product category and target market: HS code identification, import duty in target country, and top competing origins
  Develop a complete export business plan: product strategy, pricing (FOB/CIF), target buyers (5 specific companies with contact details), and marketing plan
  Create a sample export documentation set: pro forma invoice, packing list, CO application, and shipping bill draft
  Calculate export P&L: total landed cost to buyer, landed cost vs. competing origins, and net margin at different Incoterms
  Present the export business plan to a mock FIEO/bank panel: market opportunity, competitive advantage, financial projections, and government support plan

  Licensing revenue model: building a licensing business (IP holding company licensing to operating companies), and IP monetization without manufacturing
  Cross-licensing: exchanging IP licenses between companies (common in tech industry to avoid costly litigation), and creating defensive patent portfolios
  Trade secret management: NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) -- key clauses (definition of confidential info, exclusions, duration, return of materials), and maintaining trade secret status (access controls, employee training)

  Location selection for fitness: catchment area analysis (target age group density), competition mapping (existing gyms within 2km), accessibility, and parking
  Licensing: local municipal trade license, fire NOC, building use certificate, and music licensing (PPL India for playing music in commercial spaces)
  Fitness staff: certified personal trainers (ACE, NASM, ACSM certifications), group fitness instructors (Zumba, yoga certification), and front desk/sales staff
  Membership pricing: monthly, quarterly, annual (discounted), couple/family packages, and day pass -- and membership management software (Mindbody, Zen Planner, Fitbudd)

Month 2 - Marketing, Coaching & Operations
> Sports & Fitness Marketing
  Fitness marketing: transformation stories (before-after, member success stories), free trial class (lower barrier to join), and challenge-based campaigns (21-day challenge)
  Social media for fitness: Instagram (workout videos, trainer profiles, member features), YouTube (free workout content for organic reach), and Strava/fitness apps community
  Corporate fitness contracts: B2B corporate wellness programs (annual contracts with companies), on-site fitness classes, and health challenge programs
  Sports academy marketing: school tie-ups (after-school sports programs), summer camps, and open trials (talent identification program for advanced players)
  Retention marketing: member engagement (birthday wish, progress milestones), win-back campaigns (lapsed member reactivation), and referral program (bring a friend month)
> Coaching Operations
  Sports coaching methodology: skill periodization (annual training plan -- preparation, competition, transition phases), drills library, and age-appropriate training for junior athletes
  Fitness programming: strength and conditioning program design (periodization -- linear, undulating), group class programming (variety, progression), and personal training protocols
  Technology for coaching: video analysis (Hudl, Coach's Eye for sports), fitness tracking (Garmin, Polar HRV data), and AI-assisted training load monitoring
  Sports injury prevention: warm-up protocols, movement screening (FMS -- Functional Movement Screen), and return-to-sport protocols after injury
  Competitions and events: organizing internal competitions (member tournaments, fitness challenges), and participating in external events (marathons, sports leagues) as marketing

Month 3 - Scaling & Capstone
> Scaling Sports Business
  Gym chain expansion: second location decision (unit economics must be proven), standardizing the experience, and area developer/franchise model
  Online fitness scaling: subscription platform (app or web), live classes (Zoom, Moxie), on-demand library, and global audience (English instruction for international subscribers)
  Sports tech opportunities: performance analytics platforms, wearable-integrated coaching, esports (fastest growing, low infrastructure), and fantasy sports business models
  Sports nutrition business: building a brand (protein, BCAA, pre-workout), manufacturing (own plant vs. third-party contract manufacturing), and direct-to-athlete sales
  Talent management agency: representing athletes for endorsements, events, and career management -- building a roster of regional athletes with national potential
> Capstone - Sports Business Plan
  Design a complete sports or fitness business plan: specific niche, target customer (age, income, aspiration), location or digital model, and capacity plan
  Create a facility layout or service blueprint: equipment/space allocation, staffing plan, and customer journey map
  Financial model: startup costs, monthly fixed and variable costs, membership revenue projection, and break-even membership count
  Marketing launch plan: pre-opening (waitlist, founding member offer), launch week (events, trial classes), and first 90 days (retention + new member acquisition)
  Present the sports business plan to a mock investor, covering: market opportunity, competitive advantage, financial projections, team, and 3-year expansion plan
