Course #E46: Startup Incubation & Accelerator Program
Domain
Startup Ecosystem & Venture Building
Duration
6 Months
Fees
Rs.49,999

Month 1 - Idea Validation & Problem-Solution Fit
> Startup Ideation Framework
  Problem-first ideation: identifying problems worth solving (large market, acute pain, poor existing solutions, personal insight), and avoiding solution-first thinking
  Idea validation frameworks: Lean Canvas (9-block one-page business model), Business Model Canvas, and Mom Test (customer interview technique to avoid false positives)
  Market sizing: TAM (Total Addressable Market), SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market), SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) -- bottom-up vs. top-down sizing
  Competitor mapping: direct (same solution), indirect (different solution, same problem), substitute (alternative behavior), and white space identification
  India startup ecosystem: Startup India registration benefits (tax exemption, IPR fast-track, self-certification), DPIIT recognition process, and startup India portal
> Customer Discovery
  Customer segments: defining the primary customer (who pays), secondary customer (who uses), and influencer (who recommends) -- and their different motivations
  Interview methodology: 30+ customer discovery interviews, open-ended questions ("Tell me about the last time you faced X"), avoiding leading questions, and synthesis patterns
  Persona development: building data-driven proto-personas from interview patterns, and validating with quantitative survey to 200+ potential customers
  Jobs-to-be-done deep dive: functional job (what they're trying to accomplish), emotional job (how they want to feel), and social job (how they want to be perceived)
  Pivot triggers: when to pivot (insufficient traction, pattern of wrong customer responses, better opportunity discovered), pivot types, and conducting a structured pivot

Month 2 - Business Model Design & MVP
> Business Model Innovation
  Revenue model selection: subscription (predictable, loyal), transactional (volume-dependent), marketplace (network effects), freemium (wide top of funnel), and razor-blade (subsidized hardware, paid consumables)
  Pricing strategy: value-based pricing (willingness to pay research), penetration pricing (low to gain share), premium pricing (for quality signal), and dynamic pricing
  Unit economics from day one: LTV calculation (ARPU × gross margin × average lifetime), CAC (fully-loaded -- including salaries, tools, overhead), and LTV:CAC target (>3x)
  Financial modeling: building a 3-year bottom-up financial model (hiring plan → costs → revenue assumptions → P&L → cash flow → runway)
  Scenario planning: base, bull, bear cases -- and identifying the key assumptions that most affect the outcome (sensitivity analysis)
> MVP Development
  MVP philosophy: the minimum feature set that tests your riskiest assumption -- not the smallest possible product, but the most learning-efficient product
  MVP types: landing page (test demand before building), concierge (manual service before automation), wizard of oz (fake automation), prototype (clickable Figma), and paper prototype
  Build vs. buy vs. partner: for non-core features, using APIs (Stripe for payments, Twilio for SMS, SendGrid for email), no-code tools (Bubble, Glide, Webflow), and freelancers
  Tech stack selection for early startups: MERN (MongoDB, Express, React, Node) for web, React Native or Flutter for mobile -- and when to care about technology choices
  Launch and feedback loop: soft launch (50-100 users), structured feedback collection (in-app NPS, user interviews), and rapid iteration cycles (weekly sprints)

Month 3 - Go-to-Market & Early Traction
> Go-to-Market Strategy
  GTM strategy framework: who (ICP -- Ideal Customer Profile), what (value proposition and messaging), where (channels), how (sales motion), and when (launch timing)
  Channel selection: inbound (content, SEO, social) vs. outbound (cold email, cold call, LinkedIn outreach), and paid (search, social, display) -- prioritizing by CAC and conversion
  Product-led growth (PLG): free trial, freemium, self-serve onboarding, and viral loops (collaboration, sharing, referral) -- examples (Slack, Notion, Calendly)
  Community-led growth: building a community around the problem (not the product), providing value before selling, and converting community members to customers
  Launch playbook: building waitlist (Product Hunt, BetaList, LinkedIn), launch day tactics, press outreach, and first 100 customers strategy (do things that don't scale)
> Early Traction & Metrics
  North Star Metric: the single metric that best captures the core value delivered to customers -- and secondary metrics (inputs) that drive it
  Pirate metrics (AARRR): Acquisition (how users find you), Activation (first value experience), Retention (do they come back?), Referral (do they tell others?), Revenue (do they pay?)
  Retention analysis: cohort retention curves (D1, D7, D30 retention), churn analysis (why users leave), and identifying the "aha moment" (when users find core value)
  Experiments and A/B tests: structured experiment framework (hypothesis, variant, control, metric, duration), and building a culture of experimentation
  Investor traction metrics: what investors look for at pre-seed (problem validation, team), seed (early PMF signals, initial revenue), and Series A (strong retention, clear GTM)

Month 4 - Fundraising & Investor Relations
> Fundraising Strategy
  Fundraising landscape India: angels (individual, Rs.5L-50L), angel networks (Mumbai Angels, Indian Angel Network, LetsVenture), seed funds (Blume, Kae, Antler), and VCs (Sequoia Surge, Nexus, Accel)
  Funding stages: pre-seed (idea + team, Rs.25L-2Cr), seed (MVP + initial traction, Rs.1-10Cr), Series A (PMF + repeatable GTM, Rs.25-100Cr), and Series B+ (scaling)
  Instruments: equity (straightforward ownership), SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity -- Y Combinator standard), and convertible notes (India: compulsorily convertible preference shares -- CCPS)
  Valuation at early stage: pre-revenue valuation methods (scorecard, Berkus, risk factor summation), and negotiation dynamics (founder vs. investor leverage)
  Alternative funding: government grants (DST, BIRAC, Startup India Seed Fund Scheme -- Rs.20L-1Cr), revenue-based financing (Velocity, Recur Club), and accelerators (Y Combinator, Antler India, Sequoia Surge, 100X.VC)
> Pitch Deck & Investor Outreach
  Pitch deck structure: Problem, Solution, Market Size, Product, Traction, Business Model, Team, Competition, Financials, Ask -- and what investors focus on most
  Storytelling in pitch: the narrative arc (hero's journey for founder story), making investors feel the problem, and ending with a compelling vision
  Traction slide: showing momentum (MoM growth), key metrics (GMV, ARR, users, NPS), and customer logos/quotes
  Financial slide: 3-year projections (revenue, burn, runway), use of funds (hiring plan + marketing + product), and key milestones to achieve with this round
  Investor outreach: warm intro vs. cold email, researching investor portfolio fit, and follow-up cadence (persistent but not desperate)

Month 5 - Team Building & Organizational Design
> Founding Team Dynamics
  Co-founder relationship: complementary skills (tech + business, product + sales), alignment on vision and values, conflict resolution mechanism, and vesting schedule (4-year, 1-year cliff -- standard)
  First key hires: founding engineer (full-stack, pragmatic), head of growth/marketing, first salesperson -- and hiring for startup stage vs. experience at large companies
  Equity for early employees: ESOP pool (10-15% at seed stage), vesting schedule, exercise price (fair market value, usually low at early stage), and liquidation preference implications
  Culture as competitive advantage: defining values (behaviors, not platitudes), hiring for culture add (not just culture fit), and rituals that reinforce culture (all-hands, retrospectives)
  Remote and hybrid teams: async communication (Slack, Notion, Loom), documentation culture, and managing a distributed team across India (time zones, connectivity)
> Legal & Compliance for Startups
  Company structure: private limited company (recommended for funded startups -- investor-friendly, allows ESOP), LLP (for consulting, lower compliance), and one-person company
  Shareholder agreement: key clauses (anti-dilution, pro-rata rights, information rights, drag-along, tag-along, ROFR), and founder vs. investor negotiation
  IP assignment: ensuring all IP created by founders and employees is assigned to the company (invention assignment agreement), and open-source license compliance
  Startup India and DPIIT: registration process, benefits (80IC tax exemption for 3 years, IPR fee reduction, fast-track exit), and inter-ministerial board recognition
  Employment contracts: offer letter, employment agreement (IP assignment, non-compete -- limited enforceability in India, NDA), and ESOP grant letter

Month 6 - Scaling & Exit Strategy
> Scaling the Startup
  Scaling readiness: PMF confirmed (retention flattens, users pull product), unit economics work (LTV:CAC > 3x, payback < 12 months), and repeatable GTM motion
  Scaling channels: from 100 to 10,000 customers -- which channels that worked at small scale fail at large scale (referral caps out, cold outreach doesn't scale), and channel expansion
  Organizational scaling: from flat (everyone reports to founder) to functional (heads of product, engineering, sales, marketing) to divisional (business units) -- and when each transition happens
  International expansion: expanding to Southeast Asia (similar markets), Middle East (Indian diaspora), or US (largest market) -- playbook for geographic expansion
  Building a board: why startups need a board (governance, strategy, fundraising advice), board composition (founders + investors + independents), and running effective board meetings
> Capstone - Investor-Ready Startup
  Complete a full startup pitch package: executive summary (1 page), pitch deck (12-15 slides), financial model (3-year with assumptions), and data room setup
  Conduct mock investor due diligence: present to a panel of real investors/mentors, receive critical feedback, and iterate based on feedback
  Document the customer discovery and validation journey: interview summary, pivot history, and evidence of PMF
  Build a 12-month operating plan post-funding: quarterly OKRs, hiring plan, product roadmap, and marketing calendar
  Present the complete investor-ready startup to a live audience of investors, entrepreneurs, and mentors at a Demo Day event


Course #E50: CEO Excellence & Executive Leadership Program
Domain
Executive Leadership & CEO Development
Duration
6 Months
Fees
Rs.59,999

Month 1 - CEO Role & Strategic Leadership
> The CEO Mindset
  CEO vs. founder: the hardest transition in a startup's life -- from building to enabling, from doing to leading, and from expert to generalist
  CEO job description: setting direction (vision, strategy), building the team (hiring, culture), allocating resources (capital, talent, time), and representing the company externally
  Time management for CEOs: "Maker vs. Manager" schedule (Paul Graham), CEO time audit (how you spend time vs. how you should), and protecting deep work time
  Decision-making frameworks: RAPID (Recommend, Agree, Perform, Input, Decide), decision rights (who decides what), and separating reversible (fast) from irreversible (careful) decisions
  CEO psychology: dealing with loneliness at the top, managing anxiety and uncertainty, finding peer support (CEO roundtables, YPO, EO), and building resilience
> Strategic Planning
  Strategic planning process: external analysis (PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces, competitive intelligence), internal analysis (SWOT, core competencies, resource audit)
  Vision and mission: crafting a 10-year vision (BHAG -- Big Hairy Audacious Goal), 3-year picture (vivid description of success), and annual goals
  Strategy frameworks: Blue Ocean Strategy (value innovation, creating uncontested market space), Ansoff Matrix (market/product growth options), and Porter's generic strategies
  OKR system for the organization: company OKRs → department OKRs → individual OKRs, quarterly planning rhythm, and avoiding OKR theater (compliance without commitment)
  Strategic communication: cascading strategy to the organization (all-hands, manager meetings, written strategy memo), and ensuring alignment without creating rigidity

Month 2 - Financial Leadership & Capital Allocation
> Financial Acumen for CEOs
  Financial statements mastery: P&L (revenue model, cost structure, operating leverage), balance sheet (working capital management, asset efficiency), and cash flow (operating cash flow is the real profitability measure)
  Key financial metrics by stage: pre-revenue (burn rate, runway), early revenue (ARR, MRR, gross margin), growth (CAC, LTV, payback, NRR), and mature (EBITDA margin, FCF conversion, ROIC)
  Financial forecasting: rolling 13-week cash flow forecast (treasury management), annual budget process (zero-based vs. incremental), and quarterly reforecast
  Working capital management: AR (invoicing promptly, collections process, factoring), AP (payment terms optimization), and inventory (if applicable) -- cash conversion cycle
  Audit and compliance: statutory audit requirements (Companies Act), tax compliance (advance tax, TDS, GST), and building a finance team (CFO, controller, FP&A)
> Capital Allocation & Investor Relations
  Capital allocation framework: investing in growth (R&D, sales, marketing), maintaining core (operations, infrastructure), and returning capital (buybacks, dividends -- rare for growth companies)
  Fundraising strategy: when to raise (before you need it), how much (18-24 months runway), from whom (strategic vs. financial investors), and at what terms
  Board management: board meeting preparation (pre-read, materials), conducting effective board meetings (discussion-heavy, not presentation-heavy), and managing difficult board members
  Investor communication: monthly/quarterly investor updates (transparency builds trust), managing investor expectations (under-promise, over-deliver), and crisis communication with investors
  M&A strategy: acquiring for talent (acqui-hire), technology, customers, or distribution -- and M&A process (thesis, target identification, due diligence, integration planning)

Month 3 - People Leadership & Culture
> Executive Team Building
  Building the C-suite: when to hire a COO (when operations are complex), CFO (before Series B), CTO (when tech is core), CMO (when brand/marketing is strategic), and CPO
  Executive hiring: using executive search firms (retained vs. contingency), structured interviews for senior roles, and reference checks (backdoor references are critical)
  Managing executives: setting clear outcomes (not activities), CEO-executive 1:1 structure, managing underperforming executives (direct feedback, PIP, parting ways), and executive team dynamics
  Compensation for senior leaders: market benchmarking (Radford, Aon Hewitt surveys), equity vs. cash trade-off, and retention mechanisms (cliff vesting, special retention grants)
  Succession planning: identifying internal leadership potential, developing successors (stretch assignments, mentoring), and CEO succession (board-level responsibility for public companies)
> Culture & Organizational Health
  Culture defined: "the way we do things around here" -- and why culture is the CEO's primary product at scale (Patrick Lencioni, Edgar Schein)
  Culture assessment: Organizational Health Index (McKinsey), culture surveys (eNPS, Gallup Q12), and qualitative culture diagnosis (Glassdoor, exit interviews, team observations)
  Building psychological safety: Amy Edmondson's research, leader behaviors that create safety (modeling vulnerability, inviting input, responding non-defensively), and team norms
  Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DEI): business case for diversity (McKinsey research), DEI strategy (not just hiring quotas), and inclusion practices that make diverse talent stay
  Change management: leading large-scale change (Kotter's 8-step model), communicating change (ADKAR model -- Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement), and managing resistance

Month 4 - Sales, Marketing & Customer Leadership
> Revenue Leadership
  CEO as Chief Revenue Officer: in early stages, the CEO sells -- using the CEO role to open doors, build relationships, and close strategic deals
  Sales strategy: transactional (high volume, low touch, inside sales) vs. enterprise (low volume, high touch, field sales), and building the right sales motion for your product
  Sales team structure: SDR (lead generation), AE (closing), CSM (retention), and VP Sales -- when to hire each, and how to build a sales management layer
  Revenue operations (RevOps): aligning sales, marketing, and customer success around revenue, single source of truth (CRM), and revenue reporting cadence
  Pricing power: building pricing power through product differentiation, switching costs, and network effects -- and raising prices without losing customers
> Marketing & Brand Leadership
  CEO as brand ambassador: thought leadership (LinkedIn, speaking, press), personal brand vs. company brand, and the "founder effect" on company reputation
  Marketing strategy: brand marketing (long-term, awareness, trust) vs. performance marketing (short-term, measurable, scalable) -- and the portfolio approach
  Content strategy at scale: editorial calendar, content distribution (owned, earned, paid), and building a content machine (internal team vs. agency)
  PR and media relations: building journalist relationships, crafting press-worthy stories (product launches, funding, milestones, research), and crisis PR
  Digital marketing sophistication: multi-touch attribution, marketing mix modeling, brand lift studies, and measuring the unmeasurable (brand equity)

Month 5 - Operations, Technology & Risk
> Operational Excellence
  Operating cadence: daily standup (optional for executives), weekly leadership team meeting, monthly business review (MBR), quarterly business review (QBR), and annual planning offsite
  KPI dashboard design: company scorecard (financial + operational + customer + people KPIs), leading vs. lagging indicators, and data-driven decision culture
  Process excellence: Lean Six Sigma at the leadership level (identifying waste, improving flow), and when to invest in process improvement vs. staying flexible
  Outsourcing and partnerships: strategic outsourcing (non-core activities), partnership types (technology, distribution, co-marketing), and managing partner relationships
  Operational risk management: risk register (identify, assess, mitigate, monitor), business continuity planning, and supply chain resilience (multi-source strategy)
> Technology & Digital Leadership
  Digital transformation: what it means for non-tech businesses (digitizing customer experience, internal processes, and business model), and avoiding digital transformation theater
  Technology governance: IT committee, technology investment criteria (ROI, strategic fit, risk), and managing technical debt at the leadership level
  Data strategy: data as a strategic asset, building data infrastructure (data warehouse, BI tools), data governance (DPDP Act compliance, data quality), and AI/ML adoption strategy
  Cybersecurity leadership: CEO's role in cybersecurity (risk appetite, budget, culture), key risks (ransomware, data breach, phishing), and incident response plan
  Emerging technology: AI impact on your industry (automation of tasks, new products, competitive disruption), and building AI capability (team, data, partnerships)

Month 6 - Governance, Exit & Legacy
> Corporate Governance & Compliance
  Board of directors: roles (fiduciary duty -- duty of care and loyalty), board committees (audit, compensation, nomination), and independent directors (Companies Act requirement for listed companies)
  Corporate governance best practices: board meeting frequency (quarterly minimum), board information rights (monthly MIS, annual report, related party disclosures), and conflict of interest management
  Regulatory compliance: Companies Act 2013 compliance calendar (board meetings, AGM, ROC filings), SEBI compliance (for listed companies), and sector-specific regulations
  Related party transactions: definition, disclosure requirements, shareholder approval, and arm's length principle -- compliance and transparency
  ESG reporting: SEBI Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report (BRSR) for top 1000 listed companies, and voluntary ESG reporting for private companies (investor expectation)
> Exit Strategy & CEO Legacy
  Exit options: IPO (public listing -- BSE/NSE, or GIFT City IFSC), strategic acquisition (sell to larger player), secondary sale (founders/early investors sell to PE), and management buyout
  IPO readiness: SEBI ICDR regulations, financial track record requirements (3 years profitability for main board), corporate governance standards, and DRHP (Draft Red Herring Prospectus) preparation
  Acquisition process: positioning for acquisition (build the right product/team/metrics), M&A process from seller side (banker selection, teaser, CIM, management presentations, due diligence, closing)
  CEO transition: CEO succession planning, handing over to a professional CEO, and founder role post-transition (executive chairman, product visionary, board member)
  Building a legacy: what leaders are remembered for (not just financial returns, but people they developed, culture they built, problems they solved), and the CEO's long-term impact
