Day 1 - Value Proposition & Customer Segments
> Crafting a Compelling Value Proposition
  Value Proposition Canvas: customer jobs (functional, social, emotional), pains, gains, and mapping product/service to each
  Differentiation types: price, quality, convenience, customization, brand/status, and performance -- selecting your axis
  Value proposition statement formula: For [target customer] who [need/opportunity], our [product] is [category] that [key benefit] unlike [alternative]
  Pain relievers vs. gain creators: articulating how specifically your product addresses each pain and creates each gain
  Testing value propositions: landing page MVP (Google Ads traffic), customer interviews, and pre-sales as validation signal
> Customer Segments & Market Sizing
  Segmentation variables: demographic, geographic, psychographic, behavioral -- and B2B firmographic segmentation
  TAM/SAM/SOM framework: Total Addressable Market, Serviceable Addressable Market, and realistic year-1 target
  Beachhead market: selecting the smallest defensible segment, winning it completely, then expanding (Geoffrey Moore strategy)
  ICP (Ideal Customer Profile): job title, company size, industry, pain intensity, and willingness to pay for B2B
  Customer persona creation: name, demographics, goals, frustrations, preferred channels, and buying triggers

Day 2 - Channels, Revenue & Cost Structure
> Channels & Customer Relationships
  Channel types: direct (website, sales team), indirect (resellers, distributors), and digital (social, email, SEO)
  Channel phases: Awareness, Evaluation, Purchase, Delivery, After-sales -- and best channel for each phase
  Owned vs. rented channels: building owned channels (email list, WhatsApp) vs. dependency on rented platforms (Instagram)
  Customer relationship types: self-service, automated, communities, personal assistance, co-creation -- fit to segment
  Customer acquisition cost (CAC) by channel: tracking CAC per source and blended CAC, and CAC:LTV target (>3:1)
> Revenue Models & Cost Structure
  Revenue model types: one-time sale, subscription (MRR/ARR), freemium, marketplace (take rate), licensing, usage-based
  Pricing psychology: anchor pricing, decoy pricing (3-tier), charm pricing (Rs.999 vs. Rs.1000), and value-based pricing
  Cost structure: fixed costs (rent, salaries), variable costs (COGS, commission), and economies of scale analysis
  Unit economics: contribution margin per unit = Revenue per unit - Variable cost per unit, and break-even units
  Gross margin targets by industry: SaaS (70-80%), e-commerce (40-60%), services (30-50%), and hardware (20-40%)

Day 3 - Key Activities, Resources & Partners
> Key Activities & Resources
  Key activities mapping: production activities, platform/network activities, and problem-solving activities -- which are core
  Resource types: physical (equipment, location), intellectual (IP, brand, data), human (key talent), financial (credit, cash)
  Build vs. Buy vs. Partner: make-or-buy analysis for activities outside core competency, and risk assessment
  Minimum Viable Resources: what resources are truly essential for the MVP phase vs. scaling phase
  Operational processes: documenting 3-5 core processes as SOPs early, and identifying automation opportunities
> Key Partnerships & Validation
  Partnership types: strategic alliances (non-competitors), coopetition (competitors cooperating), joint ventures, buyer-supplier
  Partnership value: acquiring resources, reducing risk/uncertainty, and activities you choose not to do yourself
  OEM/White-label: building on existing infrastructure vs. building from scratch -- speed and margin tradeoffs
  BMC validation: 5 customer interviews per segment, smoke test landing page, and Wizard of Oz MVP for complex products
  Full BMC presentation: creating a single-page BMC poster and presenting to 3 potential customers for feedback

Day 4 - Competitive Analysis & Lean Validation
> Competitive Landscape Analysis
  Competitor mapping: direct competitors, indirect competitors, substitutes, and potential new entrants
  Competitive advantage analysis: cost leadership, differentiation, focus strategy (Porter), and VRIO framework (Value, Rarity, Imitability, Organization)
  Blue Ocean Strategy: eliminate-reduce-raise-create (ERRC) grid and new value curve plotting
  Feature/benefit matrix: compare your offering vs. top 3 competitors on 10 dimensions customers care about
  Perceptual maps: positioning on 2 axes (price vs. quality, speed vs. cost) and finding white spaces
> Lean Validation & Iteration
  Lean Startup methodology: Build-Measure-Learn loop, pivot types (zoom-in, zoom-out, customer, platform)
  Riskiest assumption test (RAT): identifying the single assumption that, if false, kills the business model
  Concierge MVP: doing manually what the product will automate -- and learning before building technology
  Problem-solution fit: 5 customer interviews, problem ranking, and willingness to pay signal before building
  Pivoting: Slack (gaming to workplace chat), Instagram (Burbn to photo sharing), and YouTube (dating videos) case studies

Day 5 - Financial Projections & Pitch
> Financial Planning Basics
  Revenue projection: bottom-up model (units x price, not top-down market %), monthly for year 1, quarterly for years 2-3
  Expense budget: startup costs (one-time), monthly operating expenses, and 6-month runway calculation
  Break-even analysis: fixed costs / (price - variable cost per unit) = break-even units, and break-even timeline
  Cash flow statement: inflows (sales, investment), outflows (expenses), and end-of-month cash balance forecast
  Funding requirement: runway calculation (monthly burn / monthly revenue growth), and when to raise vs. bootstrap
> BMC Pitch Preparation
  One-page business plan: vision, problem, solution, target market, revenue model, traction, team, and ask
  Elevator pitch (60 seconds): hook (problem in 1 sentence), solution, who it is for, and why now
  Storytelling for business: hero journey framework (customer is the hero, product is the guide/tool)
  Pitch practice: 3-minute BMC presentation in front of cohort, feedback using Stanford design critique method
  Assignment: complete full BMC for your own business idea and present with 3 validation data points

Day 6 - Real-World Application & Certification
> Industry-Specific BMC Application
  SaaS BMC: recurring revenue, network effects, and churn as the key metric to track and reduce
  E-commerce BMC: marketplace fees, logistics costs, and customer LTV as key sustainability metrics
  Service business BMC: hourly vs. project vs. retainer pricing, productizing services, and scalability constraints
  Social enterprise BMC: triple bottom line (profit, people, planet), and impact measurement alongside revenue
  Franchise BMC: franchisor vs. franchisee model, royalty structure, and territorial exclusivity agreements
> Certification & Action Plan
  BMC software tools: Strategyzer online, Canvanizer (free), Miro BMC template, and Notion BMC database
  Iteration workflow: hypothesis board + experiment log + validated learning documentation cycle
  Lean Canvas (Ash Maurya): BMC adapted for startups -- adds Problem, Solution, Unfair Advantage, and Key Metrics blocks
  Action plan: 30-day post-program checklist (5 customer interviews, landing page live, first revenue experiment)
  Certification: Strategyzer BMC Fundamentals online test + program completion certificate from Fillip Technologies

Day 1 - Business Structures in India
> Choosing the Right Legal Structure
  Sole Proprietorship: simplest form, no separate legal entity, unlimited liability, PAN-based, and ideal for very small businesses
  Partnership Firm: Partnership Act 1932, registered vs. unregistered, partnership deed requirements, and unlimited liability
  LLP (Limited Liability Partnership): LLP Act 2008, partners have limited liability, LLP Agreement, Form 2/3 filing
  Private Limited Company: Companies Act 2013, minimum 2 directors/shareholders, separate legal entity, and limited liability
  OPC (One Person Company): single-member company, nominee requirement, conversion to Pvt Ltd at threshold, and annual compliance
> Comparison & Selection Framework
  Compliance burden comparison: Sole Prop (minimal) vs. Partnership (moderate) vs. LLP (moderate) vs. Pvt Ltd (high)
  Tax implications: pass-through taxation (Prop/Partnership) vs. corporate tax (Pvt Ltd/LLP) and dividend distribution
  Fundraising capability: Pvt Ltd can issue equity shares (VC/angel funding), LLP can only take equity from partners
  Foreign investment: FDI allowed in Pvt Ltd (with FEMA compliance), and restrictions in LLP for certain sectors
  Startup India recognition: eligibility (Pvt Ltd or LLP, <10 years, <Rs.100 Cr turnover), benefits (tax exemption, fast-track IP)

Day 2 - Company Incorporation Process
> MCA Registration Process
  Digital Signature Certificate (DSC): Class 3 DSC, Certifying Authorities (eMudhra, Sify), and documents required
  Director Identification Number (DIN): apply via MCA portal (DIR-3), documents needed, and lifetime validity
  Name reservation: RUN (Reserve Unique Name) application, prohibited words, trademark check, and SPICe+ for company formation
  SPICe+ form: Part A (name reservation), Part B (incorporation, DIN, PAN, TAN, EPFO, ESIC, Bank account, GST in single form)
  Certificate of Incorporation: CIN (Corporate Identification Number), name availability, and MCA21 portal access
> Post-Incorporation Requirements
  Share capital: authorised vs. paid-up capital, minimum paid-up (no minimum for Pvt Ltd), and stamp duty on share certificates
  Registered office: proof of address (utility bill + NOC from owner), and change of registered office procedure
  First Board Meeting: within 30 days of incorporation, items to be covered, minutes preparation, and quorum
  Statutory Registers: Register of Members, Register of Directors, Register of Charges -- maintenance requirements
  Bank account: opening current account (documents required), and first allotment of shares documentation

Day 3 - GST, PAN, TAN & Labour Registrations
> GST Registration & Compliance
  GST registration threshold: Rs.40 Lakh turnover (goods), Rs.20 Lakh (services), and mandatory cases (inter-state, e-commerce)
  GST registration process: documents, GSTIN structure (15-digit: state code + PAN + entity code + check digit)
  GST returns: GSTR-1 (outward supplies), GSTR-3B (monthly summary), GSTR-9 (annual), and due dates
  Input Tax Credit (ITC): eligible vs. ineligible credits, time limit for claiming ITC, and reconciliation with GSTR-2B
  E-invoicing: applicability threshold (Rs.5 Cr turnover), IRN generation, QR code, and B2B e-invoice mandate
> Labour & Other Registrations
  Shops & Establishment Act: state-specific registration, working hours, leave, and closure notice requirements
  ESIC (Employees State Insurance): applicable if 10+ employees, employer contribution (3.25%), employee (0.75%)
  EPFO (Provident Fund): applicable if 20+ employees, employer contribution (12%), and Universal Account Number (UAN)
  Professional Tax: state-specific (Maharashtra, Karnataka etc.), slabs, and monthly payment to state government
  MSME Udyam Registration: free online registration, benefits (priority lending, collateral-free loans, GeM seller preference)

Day 4 - Intellectual Property & Contracts
> Intellectual Property Protection
  Trademark registration: classes of goods/services (45 classes), online application (IP India portal), and TM vs. R symbol
  Trademark search: IP India public search, identifying conflicting marks, and conducting clearance before filing
  Copyright: automatic protection (no registration needed), registration benefits (prima facie evidence), and works covered
  Patent: types (utility, design, plant), patentability criteria (novelty, non-obvious, utility), and basic application process
  Trade secret protection: NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement), internal access controls, and not sharing with former employees
> Business Contracts Essentials
  Essential contract elements: offer, acceptance, consideration, legal capacity, and free consent (no coercion/fraud)
  Founder Agreement: equity split, vesting schedule (4 years, 1-year cliff), roles, and IP assignment clause
  NDA types: unilateral vs. mutual, confidentiality definition, exclusions, and term (typically 2-5 years)
  Client contract: scope of work, payment terms (milestone-based), intellectual property ownership, and limitation of liability
  Employment vs. freelancer: control test for classification, consequences of misclassification, and contractor agreement essentials

Day 5 - Compliance Calendar & Financial Compliance
> Annual Compliance Requirements
  ROC compliance: Annual Return (MGT-7), Financial Statements (AOC-4), Board meetings (minimum 4 per year) requirements
  Director KYC: DIR-3 KYC annual filing (September deadline), and consequences of non-compliance (DIN deactivation)
  Statutory audit: mandatory for all companies, appointment of auditor (Form ADT-1), and auditor independence requirements
  Tax audit: applicability (turnover >Rs.1 Cr for business, Rs.50 Lakh for profession), Form 3CD, and CA certificate
  Event-based filings: change of directors (DIR-12), allotment of shares (PAS-3), and charge creation (CHG-1 within 30 days)
> Financial Compliance & Penalties
  Income Tax: advance tax schedule (15% by June 15, 45% by Sep 15, 75% by Dec 15, 100% by Mar 15) for companies
  TDS: applicable sections (194C contractors, 194J professionals, 194H commission), rates, and quarterly return (26Q/27Q)
  Transfer pricing: arm-length pricing for transactions with related parties, documentation, and Form 3CEB
  Penalty framework: late filing penalties (ROC filings), interest on delayed tax payments, and prosecution for serious defaults
  Compliance calendar: monthly, quarterly, annual task list for a typical Pvt Ltd company -- a practical one-page reference

Day 6 - Practical Filing & Certification
> Hands-On Portal Practice
  MCA21 v3 portal: account setup, company search, master data view, and filing e-forms with DSC
  GST portal: profile setup, return filing simulation (GSTR-3B), ITC ledger, and e-invoice generation demo
  Income Tax portal: ITR filing for business (ITR-3, ITR-5, ITR-6), tax payment (Challan 280), and TDS filing
  Udyam portal: online registration walkthrough, certificate download, and updating information
  Startup India portal: recognition application, certificate, and accessing benefits (DPIIT recognition)
> Practical Tools & Certification
  Compliance management tools: Vakilsearch, Razorpay Rize, Cleartax for GST/ITR, and MyLegalAdvice for startups
  DIY vs. CA/CS: when to hire a professional, typical CA fees for compliance, and finding a good startup-friendly CA
  Common mistakes: not filing zero-returns (GST), missing DIN KYC, not issuing share certificates, and informal equity splits
  Case study: walk through full compliance journey for a 6-month-old Pvt Ltd startup
  Certification: program completion certificate and compliance checklist tool from Fillip Technologies

Day 1 - Company Law & MCA Overview
> Companies Act 2013 Framework
  Key definitions: company, body corporate, holding/subsidiary, associate company, and related party under Companies Act
  Types of companies: public, private, OPC, small company, Section 8 (not-for-profit), Nidhi, and producer company
  MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs): role of MCA, Registrar of Companies (ROC) jurisdiction, and NCLT/NCLAT
  Company classification: based on liability (limited by shares, guarantee, unlimited), and based on number of members
  Recent amendments: Companies Amendment Act 2020, decriminalization of offences, and ease of doing business improvements
> Memorandum & Articles of Association
  Memorandum of Association (MOA): clauses (name, registered office, objects, liability, capital, subscription), and alteration
  Main objects vs. incidental objects: importance of broad objects clause for future business activities
  Articles of Association (AOA): internal management rules, share transfer restrictions, meeting procedures, and voting rights
  Model Articles: Table F for companies limited by shares -- when to use model articles vs. custom articles
  Alteration of MOA: special resolution, ROC approval for object clause change, and Central Government approval for name change

Day 2 - Incorporation Process Deep Dive
> Step-by-Step Incorporation
  Pre-incorporation checklist: director DSCs, proposed name options, address proof, and capital structure decision
  SPICe+ (INC-32): integrated form for name + DIN + incorporation + PAN + TAN + GSTIN + bank account (zero-stamp states)
  AGILE-PRO-S: linked with SPICe+ for GSTIN, EPFO, ESIC, Professional Tax, and bank account simultaneously
  INC-9: declaration by subscribers and first directors, self-declaration, and professional certification requirement
  Certificate of Incorporation: reading the certificate (CIN structure, date of incorporation, jurisdiction), and what comes next
> First 30 Days Post-Incorporation
  First Board Meeting: Notice (at least 7 days), agenda (register office, appoint bankers, first auditor), and minutes
  First Auditor appointment: within 30 days, Board resolution, ADT-1 filing on MCA (within 15 days of AGM)
  Share allotment: Board resolution, share application money, Form PAS-3 within 30 days, and share certificates (SH-1)
  Statutory registers: setup Register of Members (MGT-1), Directors and KMP (MBP-1), Charges (CHG-7) -- format and maintenance
  Company seal: optional after 2015 amendment, but useful for formal documents -- storage and custody procedures

Day 3 - Board Meetings & General Meetings
> Board Meetings Compliance
  Board meeting requirements: minimum 4 per year, gap not exceeding 120 days, and First Board meeting within 30 days
  Notice requirements: 7 clear days written notice, agenda items, and shorter notice for urgent matters
  Quorum: 1/3rd of total strength or 2 directors (whichever is higher), and quorum at every item of business
  Minutes: recording within 30 days, signing by Chairman, entering in Minutes Book, and inspection rights
  Video conferencing participation: permitted for most items, excluded items (UPSI before announcement, committee decisions), and attendance log
> Annual General Meeting
  AGM requirements: first AGM within 9 months of year-end, subsequent AGMs within 6 months, and gap not exceeding 15 months
  AGM business: ordinary business (accounts, auditor appointment, dividend) and special business items
  Notice of AGM: 21 clear days, explanatory statement for special business, and shorter notice consent (95% members)
  Resolutions: ordinary resolution (simple majority), special resolution (3/4 majority), and Section 180 approvals
  e-AGM: SEBI circular for listed companies, MCA guidance for Pvt Ltd, and virtual meeting platform requirements

Day 4 - ROC Forms & Annual Compliance
> Key ROC Forms
  MGT-7 (Annual Return): members list, shareholding pattern, debt, board changes -- due within 60 days of AGM
  AOC-4 (Financial Statements): balance sheet, P&L, directors report, auditor report -- due within 30 days of AGM
  ADT-1 (Auditor Appointment): every year at AGM, within 15 days of AGM, and Form ADT-3 for resignation
  DIR-12 (Director changes): appointment (32 days), resignation (30 days), and disqualification reporting
  MGT-14 (Board Resolutions): certain special resolutions and agreements must be filed within 30 days
> Event-Based Filings
  Share allotment: PAS-3 within 30 days, Return of Allotment, and share certificate issuance within 2 months
  Charge creation: CHG-1 within 30 days of creation, charge satisfaction (CHG-4), and ROC charge register
  Registered office change: INC-22 within 30 days, INC-23 for state change (approval required), and publication requirement
  Director address change: DIR-3, DIR-6 for KYC -- annual KYC (DIR-3 KYC) by September 30 each year
  Form 20A (Commencement of Business): within 180 days of incorporation, declaration by director, and bank statement proof

Day 5 - Strike Off, Revival & Special Compliance
> Company Strike Off & Revival
  Voluntary strike off: STK-2 form, conditions (nil liabilities, no pending litigation), and board/shareholder resolutions
  NCLT-based voluntary winding up: liquidator appointment, liquidation process, and dissolution order
  ROC-initiated strike off: grounds (non-filing, dormancy), notice (STK-1), and consequences for directors
  Revival of struck-off company: NCLT application (Section 252), time limit (20 years), and restoration procedure
  Director disqualification: Section 164 (default for 3 consecutive years), Form DIR-8 declaration, and DIN deactivation
> Dormant Company & Small Company Benefits
  Dormant company status: INC-20A lapse, application for dormant status (MSC-1), and reduced compliance
  Small Company: paid-up capital <Rs.4 Cr AND turnover <Rs.40 Cr -- relaxations (MGT-7A, no mandatory rotation of auditor)
  One Person Company compliance: MGT-7A, AOC-4, and mandatory conversion to Pvt Ltd on exceeding threshold
  LLP compliance: Form 8 (Statement of Account), Form 11 (Annual Return), and difference from company compliance
  MSME dispute resolution: MSME Samadhaan portal for delayed payments, 45-day payment mandate, and filing process

Day 6 - MCA Portal Practice & Certification
> MCA21 Portal Hands-On
  MCA21 v3 portal: business user registration, company search (CIN lookup), and director details (DIN search)
  Filing simulation: practice SPICe+ form walkthrough, MGT-7 data entry, and AOC-4 attachment requirements
  Payment modes: online payment (credit card/UPI) vs. offline (challan), fee calculator for forms, and SRN tracking
  DSC attachment: attaching DSC using emSigner utility, troubleshooting common DSC errors, and extension of emSigner
  CS/CA professional: understanding CS (Company Secretary) role, when to hire a CS, and typical retainer costs
> Compliance Tools & Certification
  Compliance software: Cleartax Corporate, ClearOne, Taxmann, and CS Professional for ROC compliance management
  Compliance calendar: monthly (DIR-3 KYC, TDS payment), quarterly (advance tax), annual (MGT-7, AOC-4) for reference
  ROC inspection: public documents available on MCA (free), and professional access for detailed records
  Case study: analyze compliance history of a public company on MCA and identify any default patterns
  Certification: program completion certificate and ROC compliance checklist from Fillip Technologies

Day 1 - Investor Psychology & Pitch Structure
> Understanding Investor Mindset
  Investment thesis: how VCs build a portfolio (10x on 20%, returning the fund), and what they actually look for
  Angel vs. VC vs. Family Office: check size, decision speed, involvement level, and strategic value comparison
  Stage-based expectations: Pre-seed (idea + team), Seed (PMF signal), Series A (repeatable growth), and due diligence depth
  Portfolio thinking: investors expect most bets to fail -- your job is to sound like the 10x exception, not safe
  Indian startup ecosystem: top VCs (Sequoia India, Accel, Matrix, Blume, Elevation), angels, and accelerators (YC India, 100X.VC)
> 10-Slide Pitch Deck Structure
  Sequoia pitch deck format: problem, solution, market size, product, traction, team, business model, competition, financials, ask
  Guy Kawasaki 10/20/30 rule: 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30pt font minimum -- and why this forces clarity
  Airbnb/Uber original decks: dissecting what made them work -- simplicity, clarity, and audacity of the vision
  Slide purpose: every slide must answer one of (Why this? Why now? Why us? Why will it work?) for the investor
  Common pitch mistakes: too much text, weak problem framing, no traction, vague ask, and not knowing the numbers

Day 2 - Problem, Solution & Market Slides
> Problem & Solution Slides
  Problem slide: make investor feel the pain (customer quote, data point, your personal story), not just describe it
  Problem validation evidence: number of interviews, survey results, lost deals to "no solution," and current workarounds
  Solution slide: show, don't tell (product screenshot/demo GIF > paragraph description), and 3-second clarity test
  Unique insight: what do you know that others don't? (Contrarian insight drives the "why now" narrative)
  Before-after-bridge: clearly show customer life before your product, after, and the bridge (your product) in 3 visuals
> Market Size Slides
  TAM/SAM/SOM: build bottom-up (number of customers x price x purchasing frequency) not top-down market reports
  Market sizing mistakes: "1% of a $100B market" is red flag -- investors want to see you understand your actual beachhead
  Growing market: show the trend (why market is growing), and your timing (why this is the right moment to enter
  Category creation: if you are creating a new category, reference analogous markets and show size trajectory
  India-specific sizing: using government data (MCA, RBI, MOSPI), NASSCOM reports, and international comparable data

Day 3 - Traction, Product & Business Model
> Traction & Product Slides
  Traction metrics by stage: Pre-seed (waitlist, LOIs), Seed (MRR/users, retention), Series A (growth rate, NPS, LTV:CAC)
  Showing growth: month-over-month growth chart, hockey stick vs. linear -- and explaining dips with context
  Product slides: wireframes (pre-launch), screenshots (post-launch), or demo video embed link -- and product roadmap
  Key metrics dashboard: single most important metric (OMTM) prominently featured, and 3-5 supporting KPIs
  Social proof: notable customers, brand logos (even in pilot), awards, press coverage, and advisor logos
> Business Model & Financials
  Revenue model clarity: how you charge, who pays, how much, and unit economics (LTV, CAC, payback period)
  Gross margin: actual or projected gross margin, and path to target gross margin by year 3
  Financial projections: 3-year P&L (revenue, gross profit, EBITDA), monthly for year 1 -- and key assumptions visible
  Burn rate and runway: current monthly burn, months of runway, and use of proceeds from this raise
  Financial model credibility: bottom-up revenue build (not "if we capture 1% of market"), and sensible cost structure

Day 4 - Team, Competition & Ask
> Team & Competition Slides
  Team slide: founder-market fit (why are YOU the right people?), domain expertise, prior exits, and complementary skills
  Advisor board: relevant industry advisors, and what specific value they add (not just logos for credibility theater)
  Org chart (if needed): current team + first 5 hires post-funding, and recruitment plan for the next 18 months
  Competition slide: honest competitive matrix (feature comparison), and your defensible advantages clearly stated
  Competitive moats: network effects, data advantages, switching costs, brand, patents, and regulatory barriers
> The Ask & Closing Slide
  Investment ask: clear amount (Rs.2 Cr / $500K), not a range -- ambiguity signals you do not know your needs
  Pre-money valuation: how to think about valuation at early stage (revenue multiple, comparable rounds, dilution target)
  Use of funds: specific allocation (30% product, 40% sales, 20% marketing, 10% ops) tied to milestones achieved
  18-month milestones: what 3-5 things will this funding enable, and what metrics will you hit to raise the next round
  Contact and closing: memorable closing line restating the vision, clear contact, and QR code to deck/data room

Day 5 - Design, Delivery & Q&A Prep
> Pitch Deck Design
  Design principles: one idea per slide, visual hierarchy (headline > visual > supporting text), and consistent color palette
  Tools: Canva (startup deck templates), Google Slides (collaboration), Figma (pixel-perfect), and Pitch.com (for sharing)
  Typography: one headline font (bold), one body font (clean sans-serif), and max 2 fonts per deck
  Charts: always label your axes, use color sparingly to highlight the key point, and include data source
  Data room preparation: deck PDF, financial model, cap table, product demo, customer references, and legal documents
> Pitch Delivery & Q&A
  Delivery: 10-minute pitch target (not reading slides), eye contact, pacing (not rushing), and owning the room
  Investor Q&A preparation: top 20 questions (market size, competition, unit economics, team, CAC, churn) with crisp answers
  Data room: organize Google Drive/Notion data room, and send secure link (DocSend for tracking who views what)
  Follow-up protocol: same-day thank you email, send deck + data room link, and 2-week follow-up cadence
  Mock pitch: full 10-minute pitch with peer feedback using structured rubric (clarity, conviction, credibility)

Day 6 - Capstone Pitch & Certification
> Live Pitch Presentation
  Full pitch deck: each participant presents a complete 10-slide deck for their own venture or assigned case study
  Investor panel simulation: mentor panel gives live feedback on problem, solution, traction, team, and ask slides
  Peer critique: structured feedback form (1 thing that excited me, 1 question I would ask as an investor, 1 improvement)
  Deck iteration: 30-minute revision based on feedback, and re-presenting the improved opening 3 slides
  Video recording: record your pitch for portfolio and practice watching yourself for delivery improvements
> Fundraising Resources & Certification
  Fundraising platforms: LetsVenture, AngelList India, Tyke (retail investors), and India Accelerator programs (list)
  Termsheet basics: pre-money valuation, dilution, liquidation preference, anti-dilution, pro-rata rights, and SAFE notes
  Convertible instruments: SAFE (Y Combinator), iSAFE (India), and Convertible Note -- when to use each
  Building investor relationships: warm introductions via LinkedIn, founder referrals, and investor updates (monthly, 5 min read)
  Certification: program completion certificate + pitch deck reviewed and signed off by mentor panel

Day 1 - AI Landscape & Business Opportunities
> AI for Business: What Actually Works
  AI capabilities in 2025: language (text, code, voice), vision (images, video), and structured data (prediction, anomaly)
  AI limitations: hallucination, knowledge cutoff, no real-time data without tools, and not suitable for mission-critical decisions alone
  Where AI creates ROI: content creation (10x output), customer service (24/7 at low cost), data analysis, and code generation
  AI adoption stages: tool user, workflow integrator, AI-first business model -- and which stage to target first
  India AI opportunity: vernacular content, agriculture AI, EdTech personalization, healthcare diagnostics, and government service automation
> Identifying AI Opportunities in Your Business
  AI opportunity audit: map all tasks employees do, classify as rule-based (automatable), judgment-based (AI-augmented), creative (AI-assisted)
  ROI calculation: (time saved per week x hourly cost) - AI tool subscription cost = weekly net value
  Quick wins vs. transformation: starting with 3-5 tasks that take >2 hours/week and are repetitive
  Department mapping: AI in marketing (content), sales (lead scoring), ops (automation), finance (forecasting), and HR (screening)
  Competitive analysis: which of your competitors are using AI, what tools, and how to differentiate with better AI adoption

Day 2 - ChatGPT & Claude for Business
> Prompt Engineering for Entrepreneurs
  System prompts for business: setting AI as your "Chief of Staff" -- defining context, constraints, and communication style
  Prompt frameworks: RISEN (Role, Instructions, Steps, End goal, Narrowing), and STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
  Business writing prompts: investor email, customer proposal, vendor negotiation, and employee performance review templates
  Research prompts: competitive analysis, market sizing, customer interview synthesis, and financial model assumptions
  Iterative prompting: treating AI as a junior analyst -- brief, review, refine, and direct to produce final deliverable
> ChatGPT Advanced Features
  Custom GPTs: building a GPT for your business (customer FAQs, HR policy, product catalog assistant)
  GPT memory: context persistence across sessions, and managing memory for consistent business persona
