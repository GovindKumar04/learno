Week 1 - Idea Validation & Market Research
> From Idea to Opportunity
  Problem vs. solution thinking: start with a painful, urgent, widespread problem -- not a product idea looking for a market
  Idea evaluation: PAIN framework (Problem severity, Accessible market, Innovation edge, Next step clear)
  Customer discovery interviews: 5-question structure (current situation, pain points, current solutions, frustration, spending), and note-taking
  Desk research: Google Trends, Statista India, NASSCOM reports, and Reddit/Quora for unfiltered customer problems
  Idea matrix: rating 5 ideas on market size, pain severity, competition, founder fit, and monetizability
> Market Research & Validation
  Primary vs. secondary research: surveys (Typeform), interviews, observation vs. reports and databases
  TAM/SAM/SOM calculation: bottom-up method using known data points (number of potential customers x average spend)
  Landing page MVP: Carrd.co or Webflow no-code landing page, Google Ads test (Rs.5000 budget), and conversion rate measurement
  Smoke test results: >2% conversion = demand signal, <0.5% = pivot needed, and iterating the value proposition
  Competitive map: identifying 5 direct competitors, 5 substitutes, and positioning differentiation

Week 2 - Business Model & Legal Foundation
> Business Model Design
  Revenue model selection: SaaS, marketplace, D2C, service, franchise, or hybrid -- matching to customer behavior
  Pricing strategy: cost-plus vs. value-based vs. competitive pricing, and psychological pricing for India market
  Unit economics first principles: LTV (lifetime value), CAC (customer acquisition cost), payback period, and gross margin
  Financial projections: 12-month revenue model, expense budget, and funding requirement calculation
  Go-to-market strategy: beachhead customer segment, initial sales channel, and 90-day traction plan
> Legal Setup & Registration
  Entity type selection: Proprietorship (fast start), LLP (flexibility), or Pvt Ltd (fundable) -- for startup context
  Startup India registration: DPIIT recognition, benefits (income tax exemption 3 years, fast-track IP, and self-certify compliance)
  Bank account: current account documents, MSME Udyam registration for loan benefits, and GST registration basics
  IP protection: trademark search and application for brand name, and basic NDA for co-founders and early employees
  Founder agreement: equity split, vesting schedule (4 years, 1-year cliff), role definition, and decision-making rules

Week 3 - Product/Service Launch
> MVP Development Strategy
  MVP types: Concierge (manual), Wizard of Oz (fake automation), landing page, video (Dropbox), and single-feature product
  Feature prioritization: MoSCoW (Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have) for MVP scope
  No-code tools for MVP: Webflow (website), Glide (mobile app), Notion (database), Stripe (payments), and Calendly (bookings)
  Launch checklist: website, payment gateway, analytics (GA4), customer support (WhatsApp), and social media presence
  First 10 customers: friends and family, direct LinkedIn outreach, WhatsApp groups, and local business community
> Sales & Customer Acquisition
  Sales funnel stages: Awareness, Interest, Consideration, Intent, Evaluation, Purchase -- mapping your tactics per stage
  Cold outreach: personalized email formula (1-2-3: 1 compliment, 2 sentences on problem you solve, 3 lines on offer)
  Referral engine: asking every happy customer for 2 referrals, and incentivizing with discount or added value
  Social selling: LinkedIn for B2B (daily posts + DM outreach), Instagram for B2C (visual content + story CTAs)
  Pricing test: launch at 80% of target price to early customers, increase to full price after testimonials collected

Week 4 - Growth, Operations & Scaling Plan
> Growth Fundamentals
  Growth hacking vs. sustainable growth: viral loops (Dropbox referral), content SEO (long-term), and paid ads (scalable)
  Metrics dashboard: weekly tracking of Revenue, New Customers, Churn Rate, NPS, and Cash Runway
  Email marketing: building list from day 1 (free download lead magnet), and weekly newsletter for retention
  Customer retention: Net Promoter Score (NPS) survey monthly, churn analysis, and proactive customer success
  Referral program: simple reward (₹500 off next purchase or 1 free month) for successful referrals
> Operations & 6-Month Plan
  Operations basics: tools stack (Notion for PM, Zoho for CRM, Razorpay for payments, Slack for team)
  Hiring first employees: freelancer vs. full-time, job description writing, and culture fit in first 5 hires
  Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs): document top 5 processes in Notion before first hire
  6-month milestone plan: Month 1 (10 customers), Month 2 (20 customers), Month 3 (profitability), Month 6 (team of 3)
  Capstone: full startup plan document -- problem, solution, market, BMC, financial model, and 6-month roadmap

Week 1 - GST Fundamentals
> GST Basics & Registration
  GST structure: CGST (central), SGST (state), IGST (inter-state), UTGST -- and how they apply to transactions
  GST slabs: 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% -- and key items in each slab relevant to small businesses
  GST registration: threshold limits, voluntary registration benefits, composition scheme eligibility (turnover <Rs.1.5 Cr)
  GSTIN structure: 15-digit breakdown (state code + PAN + entity number + Z + check digit)
  Documents required for GST registration: PAN, Aadhaar, bank statement, address proof, and digital signature
> GST on Transactions
  Place of supply rules: goods (location of delivery), services (complex rules by service type), and inter-state determination
  Tax invoice: mandatory fields (GSTIN, HSN/SAC code, tax rate, tax amount, reverse charge indicator, QR code)
  Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM): applicable supplies (GTA, legal services, import of services), and self-invoicing requirement
  Input Tax Credit (ITC): eligibility (business purpose, tax invoice, received goods/services), blocked credits (Section 17(5))
  E-way bill: applicability (>Rs.50,000 inter-state, state-specific threshold), Part A/B, and validity period

Week 2 - GST Returns & E-Invoicing
> GST Return Filing
  GSTR-1: outward supplies report, B2B invoices, B2C summary, nil returns, HSN-wise summary, due dates (11th of next month)
  GSTR-3B: monthly summary return, ITC claim, net tax payment, and consequences of late filing (interest + late fee)
  GSTR-2B: auto-populated ITC statement from supplier GSTR-1, and reconciling with purchase register
  Annual return GSTR-9: filing deadline (December 31), GSTR-9C (reconciliation statement for >Rs.5 Cr turnover)
  Composition scheme returns: GSTR-4 (annual), CMP-08 (quarterly statement), and no ITC claim under composition
> E-Invoicing & E-Way Bills
  E-invoicing applicability: mandatory for >Rs.5 Cr turnover, and API integration with IRP (Invoice Registration Portal)
  IRN (Invoice Reference Number): 64-character unique hash, QR code, and validity of e-invoice
  NIC IRP portal: live demo of e-invoice generation, JSON format, and ERP integration options (Tally, Zoho, QuickBooks)
  Cancellation of e-invoice: only within 24 hours of IRN generation, and cannot amend after cancellation
  E-way bill integration: auto-generation from e-invoice, and separate e-way bill for below e-invoice threshold

Week 3 - Income Tax for Business
> Business Income Tax
  Presumptive taxation: Section 44AD (8%/6% of turnover for eligible business), Section 44ADA (50% of receipts for professionals)
  Tax audit: Section 44AB applicability (business turnover >Rs.1 Cr, professional >Rs.50 Lakh, non-presumptive)
  ITR filing for business: ITR-3 (business income in individual return), ITR-5 (partnership/LLP), ITR-6 (company)
  Advance tax: payment schedule (15% by June 15, 45% by Sep 15, 75% by Dec 15, 100% by March 15), and interest (234B/234C)
  Deductions for business: Section 37 (ordinary business expenses), depreciation (WDV method), and startup deductions (Section 80-IAC)
> TDS & Tax Planning
  TDS basics: deductor/deductee, applicable sections (194C contractor 1%/2%, 194J professional 10%, 194H commission 5%)
  TDS payment: Challan 281, due dates (7th of next month), and TDS certificate (Form 16A within 15 days of quarter end)
  Form 26AS and AIS: reconciling TDS credits, annual information statement, and resolving mismatches before filing
  Tax planning strategies: legitimate deductions (home office, car, mobile), timing of income recognition, and Section 80C for proprietors
  Advance tax vs. TDS: how TDS offsets advance tax liability, and managing cash flow for both obligations

Week 4 - Business Accounting & Tally
> Accounting Fundamentals
  Accounting equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity -- and every transaction's impact on the equation
  Double-entry accounting: debit/credit rules, journal entries, and T-account visualization for common transactions
  Financial statements: Balance Sheet (snapshot of assets/liabilities), P&L (period performance), and Cash Flow Statement
  Bank reconciliation: matching bank statement with ledger, common reconciling items, and monthly reconciliation process
  Accounting software comparison: Tally Prime, Zoho Books, QuickBooks, and Vyapar for India GST accounting
> Tally Prime for GST Business
  Tally Prime setup: company creation, GST configuration (GSTIN, state, registration type, HSN/SAC mapping)
  Purchase and sales entry: GST-compliant voucher entry, ITC ledger, and GST summary report
  Bank entry: bank payment/receipt vouchers, contra entries, and bank reconciliation in Tally
  GST reports in Tally: GSTR-1, GSTR-3B ready reports, and exporting to JSON for direct portal filing
  MIS reports: Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow, Sundry Debtors/Creditors aging -- for business decisions

Week 1 - Personal Finance & Business Finance Separation
> Financial Foundations for Entrepreneurs
  Personal vs. business finance: separate bank accounts, salary from business (even for founders), and personal financial security before business risk
  Emergency fund: 6-12 months of personal expenses liquid before starting a business -- protecting the downside
  Net worth calculation: assets (savings, property, business equity, investments) minus liabilities (loans, credit card, personal debt)
  Cash flow management: tracking monthly personal income and expenses, and identifying discretionary cuts during lean startup periods
  Financial goal setting: business target (revenue, profit), personal target (house, emergency fund, retirement), and aligning timelines
> Business Financial Structure
  Founder salary: paying yourself market rate (even if deferred), and the purpose (tax efficiency, burn tracking, financial health)
  Profit First system: allocating revenue into buckets (owner's pay, taxes, profit, operating expenses) -- Mike Michalowicz framework
  Break-even analysis: fixed costs / gross margin percentage = break-even revenue, and monthly tracking
  Cash vs. accrual accounting: cash (recognize when paid), accrual (recognize when earned) -- and India IT Act requirement
  Working capital management: accounts receivable days, accounts payable days, inventory days, and cash conversion cycle

Week 2 - Financial Projections & Modeling
> Building Financial Models
  Revenue model: units x price (retail), clients x monthly fee (SaaS), hours x rate (services), and GMV x take rate (marketplace)
  3-statement model: P&L, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow linked -- changes in P&L flow to Balance Sheet and Cash Flow
  Bottom-up vs. top-down: build revenue from customer count x conversion rate x ARPU, not "0.1% of Rs.100B market"
  Sensitivity analysis: changing key assumptions (price, volume, churn) and seeing impact on profit and cash
  Google Sheets financial model: template with assumptions tab, revenue build, expense budget, and summary statements
> Funding & Investment Planning
  Bootstrap vs. funding: advantages (control, no dilution) vs. disadvantages (slow growth, personal risk)
  Funding stages: Pre-Seed (idea, Rs.5-25 Lakh), Seed (PMF, Rs.25L-2Cr), Series A (growth, Rs.5-20 Cr) -- and typical dilution
  Valuation basics: revenue multiple (SaaS 5-10x ARR), user-based, and comparable transaction analysis
  Dilution calculator: pre-money valuation + investment = post-money valuation, and ownership % after round
  Debt vs. equity: MSME loan (collateral-based, no dilution), Angel investment (equity, expertise), and when to choose each

Week 3 - Cash Flow & Working Capital
> Cash Flow Management
  Cash flow statement: operating (business operations), investing (asset purchase/sale), financing (loans/equity) -- and reading it
  Weekly cash flow tracker: cash in (collections), cash out (payments), end balance, and 4-week rolling forecast
  Receivables management: invoice immediately on delivery, clear payment terms (Net 15/30), and follow-up sequence (3-7-14 days)
  Payables optimization: extending supplier credit (Net 45/60), early payment discounts vs. cash retention
  Cash flow crisis signals: 3 consecutive months of negative cash flow, paying suppliers late, and using credit cards for operations
> Working Capital Financing
  Overdraft facility: CC (Cash Credit) limit from bank against stock/receivables pledge, interest only on drawn amount
  Invoice discounting: sell outstanding invoices to NBFCs (KredX, M1xchange) at 1-1.5% per month discount
  Supply chain finance: TReDS platform, Axis Bank invoice discounting, and receivable monetization for MSMEs
  MSME loans: PSB Loans in 59 minutes (up to Rs.5 Cr), MUDRA loans (Shishu/Kishore/Tarun), and CGTMSE coverage
  Credit score for business: CIBIL commercial score, Experian business score, and improving score over 12 months

Week 4 - Insurance, Investments & Exit Planning
> Business Insurance & Risk Management
  Types of business insurance: Keyman insurance (Rs.1 Cr term cover on key founders), Fire insurance, Marine (for goods), and D&O liability
  Health insurance: Group health insurance for employees (mandatory for 20+), and ESIC coverage for <21,000/month salary
  Professional indemnity: for consultants, lawyers, doctors, and tech companies against client claims of negligence
  Business interruption insurance: covers lost revenue during forced shutdown (fire, flood, pandemic), and India availability
  Product liability insurance: for manufacturers and e-commerce sellers, and recall insurance for consumer goods
> Exit Planning & Wealth Building
  Exit strategy options: strategic acquisition, PE buyout, IPO, management buyout, and passing to next generation
  Business valuation: EBITDA multiple (3-8x for SMEs), revenue multiple (0.5-2x), and asset-based valuation
  Shareholder agreement: right of first refusal, drag-along, tag-along, and exit waterfall provisions for investors
  Personal wealth building alongside business: NPS for retirement (tax deduction + employer contribution), PPF, and MF SIP
  Financial independence number: calculating the corpus needed to live off returns (25x annual expenses rule), and timeline

Week 1 - Are You Investment Ready?
> Investment Readiness Assessment
  Investment readiness checklist: market size (>Rs.500 Cr SAM), growth rate (>20% MoM), scalable model, and defensibility
  Founder-market fit: why this team is uniquely positioned -- domain expertise, distribution advantage, and lived experience
  Product-market fit signals: NPS >50, organic growth, users who would be "very disappointed" if product went away (>40%)
  Startup metrics by stage: Pre-seed (idea + team), Seed (10-100 customers, MRR Rs.1-10 Lakh), Series A (Rs.10-50 Lakh MRR, 15%+ MoM)
  Common investor rejections: market too small, no moat, team gap, and pre-revenue (for institutional investors)
> Building Investor Pipeline
  Investor research: AngelList India, LetsVenture, LinkedIn, Tracxn, and YourStory Funding database
  ICP for investors: thesis (sector, stage), typical check size, portfolio companies (conflict check), and preferred geography
  Warm vs. cold: warm intro (from portfolio founder or mutual connection) has 10x higher response rate than cold email
  Investor CRM: Notion or Airtable tracker (name, firm, stage, contact, status, next action, notes, intro source)
  Building relationships before fundraising: commenting on investor tweets/LinkedIn, attending events, and sharing updates

Week 2 - Fundraising Documents
> Pitch Deck Deep Dive
  Pitch deck sections: Cover, Problem, Solution, Market, Product (demo), Business Model, Traction, Team, Financials, Ask
  Traction slide: Month-over-month growth chart is the single most important slide -- show steep slope and explain dip
  Team slide: Why us? Prior exits, domain expertise, and complementary skills -- not just logos and job titles
  Competitive positioning: avoid saying "we have no competition" -- show competitors and your differentiation clearly
  One-pager (teaser): 1-page PDF for initial investor outreach -- 5 elements: problem, solution, traction, team, ask
> Financial Model & Data Room
  Investor-grade financial model: 3-year P&L, monthly year 1, key assumptions visible, and scenarios (base, bull, bear)
  Unit economics slide: LTV (by cohort), CAC (by channel), payback period, and gross margin -- with data source
  Cap table: founding team, option pool (15-20%), pre-money cap table, and post-money cap table after proposed round
  Data room setup: Google Drive or DocSend folder structure (deck, model, cap table, legal docs, customer references)
  DocSend analytics: tracking which slides investors spend time on, page-by-page analytics, and email capture for visits

Week 3 - Fundraising Process
> Running the Process
  Fundraise timeline: 3-6 months is normal -- batching meetings to create urgency and FOMO among investors
  Investor meeting: 10-minute pitch + 20-minute Q&A -- practice until numbers are instant, and prepare for deep dives
  Follow-up protocol: same-day email (thank you + deck if not sent + data room link), and 2-week follow-up cadence
  Term sheet basics: pre-money valuation, investment amount, liquidation preference (1x non-participating standard), anti-dilution
  iSAFE (India SAFE): Scribd downloadable iSAFE template, valuation cap, discount rate, and MFN clause -- for early stage
> Negotiating & Closing
  Negotiation leverage: multiple term sheets create competition, and investor FOMO (portfolio company referral timing)
  Due diligence: financial (audited accounts, MIS), legal (MOA, contracts, IP ownership), and technical (for SaaS)
  Closing documents: SHA (Shareholders Agreement), SSA (Share Subscription Agreement), and ESOP scheme approval
  Compliance post-investment: Form PAS-3 (allotment return), Form FC-GPR for FDI (foreign investment), and FEMA compliance
  Post-raise relationship: monthly investor update email (2 pages: wins, challenges, asks), and board meeting conduct

Week 4 - Alternative Funding & Grant Programs

  Startup India Seed Fund: up to Rs.20 Lakh (validation), Rs.50 Lakh (prototype), and Rs.1.5 Cr (market entry) via incubators
  SIDBI Startup Mitra: Rs.10-50 Lakh at concessional rates for Startup India recognized companies
  TIDE 2.0 (MeitY): technology innovation program, up to Rs.4.5 Lakh for IT/ITeS startups, and IT support
  State-specific schemes: Bihar Startup Policy (details), UP Startup Policy, and Maharashtra Startup Week grants
  Atal Innovation Mission: AIM grants, ATL (Atal Tinkering Labs), and Atal Incubation Centre partnerships
> Revenue-Based & Non-Dilutive Funding
  Revenue-Based Financing (RBF): Velocity, Klub, GetVantage -- 5-25% revenue share until 1.5-2.5x repaid, for Rs.10L-5Cr
  Bank loans: SBI SME loan, HDFC Business Loan, and PSB Loans in 59 Minutes platform for quick sanction
  NBFC fintech: Lendingkart, Indifi, Flexiloans -- faster than banks, higher rate, for working capital needs
  Crowdfunding: Milaap (social cause), Ketto (personal + social), Wishberry (creative), and SEBI-regulated equity crowdfunding
  Accelerators with funding: Y Combinator (global), 100X.VC (India), Antler India, and Surge (Sequoia) -- stipend + equity

Week 1 - VC & Angel Ecosystem
> How VCs & Angels Think
  VC fund structure: LP → GP → portfolio companies, carry (20%), management fee (2%), and fund lifecycle (10 years)
  Power law returns: VCs need 1 company to return the entire fund -- explaining why they only invest in high-growth startups
  Deal flow funnel: 1000 companies seen → 100 meetings → 20 term sheets → 10 investments → 1-2 breakouts per fund
  Investment thesis: sector focus (B2B SaaS, D2C, fintech), stage focus (Seed, Series A), and geographic focus (India, SEA)
  Angel investor profile: HNIs (Rs.25-50 Lakh ticket), ex-founders (Rs.5-25 Lakh), and family offices (Rs.50L-2Cr)
> India VC Landscape
  Top VCs by stage: Pre-seed/Seed (100X.VC, Antler, Blume, Arali), Series A (Accel, Matrix, Elevation, Sequoia India)
  Sector specialists: Stellaris (SaaS), Lightspeed India (consumer), Nexus (enterprise), and Kalaari (internet/mobile)
  Angel networks: Indian Angel Network (IAN), Mumbai Angels, Chennai Angels, and Let'sVenture platform for deal syndication
  Corporate VCs: Tata Capital, Axis Growth Avenues, Ola Electric Mobility, and Google for Startups India
  Unicorn factory: Bangalore (tech), Mumbai (fintech), Delhi-NCR (consumer), and Hyderabad (pharma/biotech) -- geography and ecosystem

Week 2 - Advanced Pitch & Due Diligence
> VC-Level Pitch Preparation
  Series A pitch vs. Seed pitch: Series A needs repeatable GTM, unit economics, and market leadership -- not just product
  Narrating your numbers: every metric needs context (compared to cohort, vs. market benchmark, vs. own past)
  Technical due diligence prep: system architecture diagram, tech debt documentation, engineering velocity (story points), and team structure
  Market sizing for VCs: build a narrative (India has 50M SMEs, 30% lack X, 10M are your ICP, Rs.5000 ARPU = Rs.500 Cr SAM)
  Managing investor objections: "market is too small" → show adjacencies; "too competitive" → show 10x differentiation
> Term Sheet Deep Dive
  Economics: pre-money valuation, price per share, pro-rata rights (right to invest in future rounds), and information rights
  Control: board seat composition (founders 2, lead investor 1, independent 1), veto rights (major decisions), and board observer
  Protective provisions: founder liquidity restrictions, ROFR (Right of First Refusal), drag-along (sale), and tag-along
  Anti-dilution: broad-based weighted average (startup-friendly) vs. full ratchet (investor-friendly), and carve-outs
  Employee stock option pool (ESOP): creation pre-investment (dilutes founders, not investor), option pool size negotiation

Week 3 - Investor Relations & Post-Investment
> Investor Update Best Practices
  Monthly investor update format: MoM growth, key wins, key challenges, asks (intros, advice, hires), and next month plan
  Sequoia AMP format: 1-pager with ARR, Net New ARR, Gross Margin, Net Revenue Retention, CAC/LTV, Cash, Headcount
  Celebrating milestones: sharing customer wins, press coverage, and product launches via WhatsApp/Slack investor group
  Bad news protocol: share bad news (key employee departure, major customer churn) proactively before investors hear elsewhere
  Board meeting preparation: board pack (financials, strategy, decisions needed), pre-read 2 days before, and follow-up minutes
> Building a Board & Advisors
  Board composition: 3-member board (2 founders, 1 investor) at Seed, expanding to 5 at Series A with independent director
  Independent director: choosing someone with relevant industry expertise, not a friend -- and compensation (ESOP 0.25-0.5%)
  Advisor program: formal advisor agreement (FAST agreement template), ESOP 0.1-0.5% with 2-year vesting, and 4-hour/month commitment
  Mentor network: building relationships with 5 experienced founders in adjacent spaces for pattern recognition and introductions
  Investor as partners: using VC network (talent, customers, co-investors) -- asking for introductions proactively

Week 4 - Valuation & Cap Table Management
> Startup Valuation Methods
  Pre-revenue valuation: Berkus method (idea, prototype, team, strategic relationships, product rollout -- Rs.10L each)
  Revenue-based: SaaS (5-10x ARR), marketplace (1-3x GMV), D2C (1-2x revenue), services (1-2x EBITDA)
  Comparable transactions: looking at similar stage/sector Indian startup rounds (Tracxn, Crunchbase data)
  VC method: exit value (5-year projected revenue x exit multiple) discounted at 30-50% IRR = post-money valuation today
  409A valuation equivalent: India fair market value opinion for ESOP issuance -- and selecting a valuation firm
> Cap Table & ESOP Management
  Cap table evolution: Founding (100%) → Angel round → Seed round → ESOP pool → Series A -- dilution per round
  ESOP scheme: company ESOP scheme approval (Board + Shareholders), vesting schedule, exercise price, and tax on exercise
  ESOP taxation in India: perquisite tax at exercise (on FMV - exercise price), LTCG on sale after 24 months
  Secondary transactions: founder liquidity (selling 20-30% in secondary at Series B+), and secondhand market platforms
  Cap table tools: Carta (global), trica equity (India), and Excel templates for early-stage cap table management

Week 1 - Sales Fundamentals & Prospecting
> Sales Mindset & Process
  Sales as a career: highest-paying non-technical profession, and sales skills compound into entrepreneurship and leadership
  SPIN Selling: Situation (understand context), Problem (uncover pain), Implication (amplify consequences), Need-Payoff (paint value)
  Challenger Sale: teach (insight), tailor (to stakeholder), take control (of the conversation) -- for complex B2B sales
  Consultative selling: asking questions before pitching, understanding the real underlying need vs. stated request
  Sales process design: Prospecting → Qualification → Discovery → Proposal → Negotiation → Close → Onboard → Renew
> Prospecting & Lead Generation
  Ideal Customer Profile (ICP): firmographics (company size, industry, revenue, location) + trigger events (funding, hiring, expansion)
  LinkedIn Sales Navigator: filter by ICP, save leads, and track job changes as re-engagement triggers
  Cold email formula: personalized first line (reference their LinkedIn post/company news) + value proposition + clear one ask
  Cold calling script: intro (10 sec) + reason for call (pain point) + qualifying question + next step ask -- 3-minute call
  Inbound vs. outbound: inbound (SEO, content, referrals) generates warm leads, outbound (cold email, LinkedIn) generates volume

Week 2 - Discovery, Proposal & Objection Handling
> Discovery & Proposal
  Discovery call structure: 40% questions (SPIN), 30% listening and note-taking, 20% value articulation, 10% next step
  Qualifying framework: MEDDIC (Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion)
  Proposal writing: Executive Summary (problem + solution + ROI), Scope, Timeline, Investment, Terms -- 5 pages max
  ROI calculation for proposal: quantify the problem (cost of not solving) × probability × your solution's impact = ROI
  Case study in proposal: brief customer story (same industry/size), problem, your solution, and measurable result
> Objection Handling
  "It's too expensive": acknowledge → question (vs. what?/what's your budget?) → ROI reframe → payment terms
  "I need to think about it": diagnose (what specifically?) → address specific concern → trial close → next meeting
  "We use [competitor]": acknowledge → what's working, what's not? → differentiation focus → free pilot offer
  "Not the right time": understand timeline → confirm pain is still real → stay connected (monthly value emails)
  Final objection patterns: each objection is a request for more information -- listen, diagnose, and address specifically

Week 3 - Closing & Negotiation
> Closing Techniques
  Trial close: "If we can solve [issue], would you move forward?" -- testing commitment before final close
  Assumptive close: "Should I send the agreement for next Monday start?" -- assumes decision is made
  Summary close: summarize all agreed-upon benefits, and then ask "Ready to get started?"
  Urgency creation: legitimate urgency (price increase, limited implementation slots) vs. fake urgency -- only use real urgency
  Multi-stakeholder close: identifying all decision-makers, getting consensus-buy-in, and facilitating internal champion
> Negotiation Skills
  BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement): know your walk-away point and never negotiate without an alternative
  Value vs. price negotiation: "Our price reflects [value element] -- what specifically is over your budget?"
  Negotiation tactics: anchoring (first number), bracketing, and splitting the difference (and why to avoid it)
  Concession strategy: give concessions slowly, make them feel earned, and always get something in return
  Deal structure creativity: payment terms (50/50), phased implementation, or performance-based pricing as alternatives to discount

Week 4 - Business Development & Partnerships
> Strategic Business Development
  BD vs. Sales: Sales (individual accounts), BD (channels, partnerships, platforms that multiply sales throughput)
  Partnership types: referral partners (2-5% commission), reseller (margin discount), integration (co-sell), and OEM (white-label)
  BD pitch: "I have customers who need your product/service -- here is how we work together to serve them"
  Channel partner program: recruiting criteria, training program, co-marketing, commission structure, and MDF (Market Development Fund)
  Strategic alliances: co-hosting events, joint content, co-bidding on large contracts -- building a referral ecosystem
> Account Management & Revenue Growth
  Upsell vs. cross-sell: timing (after 60-day success), trigger (product usage data), and internal champion as advocate
  Account expansion framework: land (minimal footprint), expand (add users/products), and consolidate (replace other vendors)
  Executive sponsorship: building C-level relationships in key accounts for protection against champion turnover
  Quarterly business review (QBR): prepare 30-minute QBR deck (results, ROI, roadmap, expansion plan, renewal discussion)
  Capstone: complete sales campaign -- 50-contact prospecting list, 5 discovery calls, 2 proposals, and 1 close simulation

Week 1 - Communication Fundamentals
> Science of Effective Communication
  Communication model: sender, message, channel, receiver, feedback -- and where most breakdowns occur
  Mehrabian's 7-38-55 rule: 7% words, 38% tonality, 55% body language -- and why delivery matters as much as content
  Listening types: passive (hearing), active (attention + acknowledgment), and empathic (understanding feeling + content)
  Communication styles: assertive (clear, direct, respectful), passive (avoidant), aggressive (dominating), and passive-aggressive
  Storytelling science: dopamine (anticipation), oxytocin (empathy and trust), and cortisol (tension) -- chemicals of a good story
> Voice & Delivery
  Voice modulation: pitch (high = excitement/question, low = authority/statement), pace (slow for emphasis, fast for excitement)
  Pause as power: the deliberate pause (3 seconds) communicates confidence and gives audience time to absorb
  Diction and articulation: tongue twisters for warm-up, enunciation exercises, and recording yourself for self-analysis
  Accent neutralization: Indian English clarity -- rhoticity, syllable stress, and common pronunciation patterns for global audience
  Breathing for speaking: diaphragmatic breathing, 4-7-8 breathing before presentations, and managing voice under stress

Week 2 - Presentation Skills
> Structuring Presentations
  Presentation frameworks: Problem-Solution-Call to Action, STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result), and Monroe's Motivated Sequence
  Opening techniques: startling statistic, rhetorical question, story (first 90 seconds = make or break), and audience poll
  Slide design for speakers: minimal text (5 words per bullet, 5 bullets max), full-bleed images, and presenter notes
  Closing techniques: callback to opening, 3-point summary, memorable one-liner, and clear call to action
  Practice methodology: recording every practice session, watching without sound (body language), and timing to ±30 seconds
> Managing Presentation Anxiety
  Anxiety reframe: symptoms (heart rate, adrenaline) are identical to excitement -- tell yourself "I am excited" not "I am nervous"
  Preparation cure: over-prepare content (know 2x what you will say), practice the first 2 minutes until automatic
  Power posing: 2 minutes of expansive posture before speaking -- Amy Cuddy's research on cortisol and testosterone
  Progressive desensitization: start with 2-person conversation, then small group, then 10 people, then 50+
  Visualization: mentally rehearsing a successful presentation (specific detail: room, faces, confident delivery, applause)

Week 3 - Business Communication Scenarios
> High-Stakes Communication
  Board presentations: BLUF (conclusion first), 10-slide maximum, anticipate top 5 questions, and pre-reading 48 hours before
  Investor pitch delivery: confidence in numbers (instant recall), managing skeptical questions, and follow-up email post-meeting
  Sales presentation: discovery before presenting, customer-language (not company jargon), and demo-driven with minimal slides
  Media interview training: bridging technique (answer then redirect to key message), flagging (marking key points), and the pause
  Town hall and all-hands: transparent (share real numbers), two-way (Q&A), and inspiring (connect work to vision)
> Interpersonal Communication
  Feedback delivery: SBI model (Situation, Behavior, Impact) for specific, actionable, and respectful feedback
  Difficult conversations: preparation (specific outcome desired), leading with empathy, using "I" statements, and collaborative solution
  Negotiation communication: active listening to understand their priority, and yes-and (not yes-but) collaborative framing
  Cross-cultural communication: high-context (India, Japan) vs. low-context (USA, Germany) -- direct vs. indirect communication
  Virtual communication: camera presence (eye contact at camera, not screen), lighting, background, and engagement cues in video calls

Week 4 - Leadership Communication
> Executive Presence
  Executive presence: gravitas (substance, assertiveness), communication (speaking up, commanding a room), and appearance (professional polish)
  Commanding a room: entering with confidence, occupying space (no shrinking), scanning the room, and speaking to the back row
  Communication in a crisis: acknowledge, express empathy, take responsibility (if appropriate), share action steps, and follow up
  Thought leadership communication: writing one opinion article per month (LinkedIn or media), and speaking at 1 event per quarter
  Storytelling for leaders: company origin story, company purpose story, and customer transformation story -- mastering all three
> Capstone & Performance Review
  Capstone speech: 5-minute prepared speech on your business or a chosen topic, recorded and peer-reviewed
  Impromptu speaking: PREP (Point, Reason, Example, Point) for 2-minute impromptu on random business topic
  Self-assessment: reviewing recorded speech against rubric (structure, delivery, content, body language, time), and identifying top 3 improvements
  360 feedback: peer feedback form (what landed well, what to improve, one memorable line from your speech)
  Action plan: one speaking opportunity in next 30 days (team meeting, client pitch, or local event), and continued practice roadmap

Week 1 - Inventory Management Fundamentals
> Inventory Basics & Classification
  Inventory types: raw material, WIP (Work In Progress), finished goods, MRO (Maintenance, Repair, Operations)

  Reorder Point (ROP): (Lead Time × Average Daily Demand) + Safety Stock -- never stockout calculation

  Economic Order Quantity (EOQ): square root of (2 × Annual Demand × Ordering Cost / Holding Cost per unit)
  Min-Max system: minimum stock level triggers reorder, maximum stock level is order ceiling -- simple for SMEs
  Just-In-Time (JIT): zero inventory goal, supplier reliability requirement, and risk (stockout on supply disruption)

  Racking systems: pallet racking (bulk), shelving (small items), drive-in racking (LIFO), and mezzanine for pick-pack
  Barcode and QR labeling: GS1 barcode standards, location labels, bin labels, and handheld scanner system setup
  5S methodology: Sort (remove unnecessary), Set in order (designate location), Shine (clean), Standardize (SOP), and Sustain (habits)
> Receiving, Put-Away & Picking
  Receiving process: PO matching (3-way match: PO, GRN, invoice), quality inspection, damage recording, and barcode scanning
  Put-away: directed put-away (WMS assigns location), bulk storage, and cross-docking for fast-moving items

  Pick accuracy: barcode confirmation scan, weight check, and photo documentation for high-value items

Week 1 - Franchise Fundamentals
> Franchise Business Model
  Franchise definition: license to use brand, business system, and support in exchange for fees (royalty + franchise fee)

  Franchise disadvantages: less autonomy, ongoing royalty (4-8% of gross revenue), territory restrictions, and contract termination risk
> Evaluating a Franchise Opportunity
  FDD (Franchise Disclosure Document): 23-item disclosure document -- finances, litigation history, franchisee contacts, and obligations
  Financial performance representation: Item 19 of FDD -- average unit volume, top/median/bottom franchisee performance
  Franchisee validation: calling 10+ current franchisees (satisfaction, support, actual vs. projected earnings, would they do it again?)
  Investment breakdown: franchise fee (Rs.5-50 Lakh), fit-out cost (Rs.10-100 Lakh), equipment, working capital, and grand total
  Break-even analysis: (monthly investment cost / gross margin %) = required monthly revenue to break even

  Operations manual: 5-volume framework (brand, operations, equipment, marketing, people) -- total 300+ pages for QSR
  System documentation: every customer touchpoint defined, trained, and audited -- the franchise is the manual
  Trademark registration: registered trademark is foundation of franchising -- trademark in all relevant classes
  Pilot franchise: running 1-2 company-owned units before franchising to test the system and gather performance data
> Franchise Legal & Financial Structure
  Franchise agreement: territory (exclusive/non-exclusive), term (5-10 years), renewal, transfer, and termination conditions
  Royalty structure: % of gross sales (4-8%), flat monthly fee, or tiered -- and typical India franchise royalty benchmarks
  Franchise fee: one-time payment (Rs.3-25 Lakh range for India SME franchise), and what it covers (training, setup support, rights)

  Franchise discovery process: inquiry → information session → site visit → FDD review → application → interview → award
  Franchisee selection criteria: net worth (2x total investment), liquid capital (30% of investment), market knowledge, and motivation

  Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM): property price / annual gross rent -- quick screening tool for investment properties

  NRI property purchase: FEMA rules, repatriation limits, and NRI home loan -- and TDS deduction on NRI property purchase (20%)
  Construction finance: project finance for developers, RERA escrow account requirement (70% collections), and construction draw schedule
